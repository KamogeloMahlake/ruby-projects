The two girls had gotten quite close in the last three months.


Both of them were genius swordswomen and Kuina being the prodigy she was, was speeding through the Breathing Method training. According to Kanae, she had never seen someone progressing so fast.


We were still not at the lead of the group.


At the forefront was Saitō who had been learning chakra techniques and breathing methods like a fish to the water. His experience with manipulating his Chi was coming in handy and his strength was increasing at a visible pace.


Right now, both him and Kuina are at Chunin level while I am at High Genin level due to lacking the skills but my physique and chakra were all as good as a top Chunin. Not to say I was helpless. I still had some tricks up my sleeves.


Kanae was still learning more about chakra so her strength was still in progress but even then she would easily be a Chunin level.


We didn’t know any good attack jutsu yet and mainly worked on the basics like breaking Genjutsu, Chakra Enhancement, Tree and Water Walking.


We were progressing slowly as we didn’t have any good teacher to teach us. Only Kensho and I had any ideas about these things and even then I was just a beginner.


As we ran further, a Samurai came running towards us.


“Your Highness!” He was relatively young and looked like he was terrified of something.


“What happened?” I asked.


“There….there are shinobi fighting a little distance outside the town.” He replied as soon as he regained his breath.


A chill ran down my spine at the words but I still asked.


“How many and how far? What village are they from? Were any of our people injured?”


“Two kilometres outside the city. A group of shinobi from Iwagakure were chasing three shinobi from Uzugakure. The Iwa group had more people and the Uzu group looked like they were in trouble.”


“Uzu and Iwa?” What are Uzu and Iwa people doing here?


“What should we do, Your Highness?” The samurai trainee asked.


I looked at Saitō. “We watch from a distance and see the situation for ourselves before deciding.”


He nodded and our main group went towards the direction of combat.


On reaching the scene, I brought out a few pairs of binoculars for my group and we looked at the fighting scene.


The three Uzumaki people were clearly identifiable from their distinct red hair. They were surrounded by twice the number of Iwa shinobi….the key word being were


They had already killed three of them while two others were seriously wounded but it was not without costing them injuries as well.


“How strong do they look to you, Kensho?” I asked, my eyes narrowing at the scene, several things going on in my head.


“They are all Chunin except two of the injured Uzumakis who might be stronger considering that they are fighting at that level even with such injuries. The dead Iwa might have been Jonins or I can’t see them being pushed to this extent.” Kensho said as he inspected the battle.


“The stronger ones are trying to protect the weakest redhead or they could probably have escaped.” Saitō remarked. “She must be someone important….at least to them.”


“The red haired ones are poisoned.” Kanae said, her usual tone replaced by a calm and serious voice. “Their movements are getting slower. The other party knows this and thus they are delaying the fight.”


“From the look in their eyes, it looks like they would sooner or later go suicidal to take down their enemies.” Saitō said.


We looked closer and observed another one of the Iwa people getting impatient and tried to sneak an attack on the weakest Uzumaki only for him to get struck by the sword and kicked away by the older ones.


The other Iwa shinobi didn’t let go of this and a kunai was lodged into the guys of the older Uzumaki in exchange.


The injured Iwa was picked up by one of his comrade while another one came forward to protect the two.


It was at that moment that we saw the older Uzumaki making a hand sign and the guy who had just been kicked away blew up.


I heard Kuina take a breath at the scene. “That’s….”


“The reason why the Uzumaki Clan are so feared in the Shinobi World. They are a Clan of Fuinjutsu Masters. True Masters could implant an Explosive Seal on your body with just a touch.”


The blast took down both the injured guy as well as the one healing him while the one protecting the two was injured badly.


Suddenly, the playing field was evened….or so it looked.


One of the injured Uzumaki suddenly vomited blood and almost kneeled.


At this rate they will all die….


Suddenly a thought came to my mind and I turned towards Saitō. “Do you think we can take the Iwa shinobi out?”


Kensho was surprised. “Your Highness?”


Saitō was not deterred and nodded.


“Good then save the Uzumaki. Take down Iwa. Attack!”


Kensho was a true samurai as even when the guy was doubtful about the orders, he still took action as soon as an order was given. There was no hesitation at all.


I observed them from a distance.


It was the first time I had seen my people fight for real.


Kanae was like a completely different person in a fight. She just tore through the Iwa’s surprised defence in a moment.


While her style was the epitome of gracefulness, Saitō’s style was like watching a surgeon. Fighting with pinpoint accuracy. When he attacked, he always got what he wanted.


Kensho and Kuina were standard Samurai through and through.


With their help, the Iwa shinobi were taken care of easily and captured.


The Uzumaki however still didn’t leave their defensive position and were guarding against us.


I decided to take the lead at this time.


“Your Highness!” Kensho noticed me coming towards them and came forward to guard me. The other group also looked at me warily.


I waved at Kensho and looked at the Uzumaki group.


“Greetings, shinobi. We are the Prince of the Land of Flowers, Kazuya Katsuyoshi. You do not need to be wary of us. The reason we ordered our people to take action was because some of these Iwa shinobi had been causing trouble in our territory as well. They stole, burned and pillaged our land and killed our people so it is only obvious that as the ruler of the Land, we should take action.” I explained. It wasn’t even a lie.


Iwa was hated by the people of the Land of Flowers due to the previous war which took the life of the previous Daimyo, my father as well as took away several parts of our lands 


They looked slightly satisfied but didn’t let down their guard completely.


This time, I observed the group closely. The two older ones were both girls, twins in fact while the youngest one was a guy.


“We know it is not easy for you to trust us here so you could follow us to our capital city and see for yourself. As it looks, you three don’t look like you can keep standing for much longer.”


The oldest looking one who looked like in her twenties came forward and bowed slightly. “We thank you for saving our lives, Your Highness. Our Uzumaki village will never forget this favour.” She then hesitated before looking back at the other two and then at us. “I know it’s asking too much but we and the entire Uzushiogakure will be eternally grateful if you could provide us a place to stay until we recover.” She bowed deeply this time.


I smiled at her words. “Obviously. If we wanted you to die, we could have waited longer before interfering. You are welcome to rest with us till you recover. Just be reminded that you need to follow the rules of the land.”


“We swear.” She quickly replied.


“Capture the surviving Iwa Shinobi and take the bodies of the dead.” I ordered as I led the three back.


Three of them were still alive although barely.


We already had two chunins captured in our cells. If I can get two more, I could do another ritual.


To be honest, I wasn’t sure I could capture Chunins so easily until my people were a bit stronger so I had left this ritual on the backburner. Soul Rituals always took an insane amount of materials.


Sure I could kill the Uzumaki too but I had no intention of doing so. I wanted to establish friendly relationships with them. They could be of much greater help if my plan works.


With this thought in mind, I began talking with them, introducing them to the city as we walked back home.

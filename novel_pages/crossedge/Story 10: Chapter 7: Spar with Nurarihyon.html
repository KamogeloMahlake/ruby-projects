That was the real you, wasn’t it?”


I looked at the man with an open mouth while he continued.


“After that I did some digging around and found out about your background. You were living with that woman, Ageha Ikuse and were getting visited by the exiled Shuri Himejima. On digging further, and putting your hatred for the Cult of Hagoromo Gitsune into account, it was easier to pinpoint your true identity, an exile of the Kushihashi Clan, the son of that Lightning Fist brat. If you want to keep your identity hidden, you should learn Senjutsu or anyone with a decent practice of Senjutsu would be able to identify you as long as they see your real form.”


I forced myself to break away from my trance and quickly concentrate on the thing he had said. “Senjutsu?”


I remember Grandma telling me about the Senjutsu of this world but she didn’t know much about it so she couldn’t elaborate further on the topic.


“Yes. Youkai are beings born of the world so they are closely connected to this world. This makes them innately compatible with the energy of the world and thus Senjutsu. Senjutsu is the art of using nature's energy for yourself. It not only allows you to fight at a much higher level but also see through the auras of other people. If you truly wish to keep your identity hidden, you’d sooner learn Senjutsu as there are very few methods of hiding from any Senjutsu user. Being one yourself is the easiest of them.”


He said as he sat down beside me and put a hand over his burned arm. The burn wound on his arm healed.


“That attack was quite vicious, you know? This is one of my favourite kimonos.” He said, a bit annoyed. “Still, I guess I might be getting too old if a kid barely out of his diapers could do this much harm to me. Sigh….now if only Rikuo took his work seriously and trained harder, I could retire with peace.”


I looked at the man who didn’t look anything like the guy who had just handed me my ass on a silver platter.


He looked just like a normal old man complaining about his grandson.


I thought of something and brought out some of the Kofuku Manju from my inventory and handed it to him.


“Oh, you had more. Good. At least you know how to apologise.” He said, taking the sweet and eating.


“Why haven’t you told Rikuo?” I asked after a while.


“Hmm? Why would I? It’s none of my business.”


“....” I looked blankly at the guy.


“You two are friends. Do whatever you want between yourself. Anyways, that brat needs to get his ass on line. Maybe the loss against you today will make him think carefully. The Nura Clan is not something one can rule over by half-assing things.”


“Thank you.”


“So….why are you hiding your identity?” He asked.


I looked at the ground below me as I remembered my life until now and let out a sigh. “I don’t have many people close to me and I don’t want people to target anyone close to me. Me revealing myself would make sure that they do so.”


“Hmm. That’s true. Is this about your father?” He asked.


I didn’t answer but sometimes silence can be the best answer.


“You do know that your father, as a part of the Five Principal Clans, has killed many youkai too. If you think about it, you are trying to do the same thing they did.”


My neck snapped in his direction and I was pretty sure I was glaring daggers at the guy.


He however seemed unphased by my glare.


“Humans kill youkai, youkai kill humans. They go after each other for revenge and the cycle of hatred continues.”


“Are you telling me to let the people who killed my father go?” I asked, my voice cold.


“Heh….that’s not my place to tell you. I am just stating what I have seen in all these years. Do you think I am lying? If this were some innocent youkai killed by your father, would you care for them?” He stared back at me with calm eyes and I was the one who averted my eyes.


“You wouldn’t be talking like this if you had lost someone close to you.” I spat in my anger. I knew it was in anger and completely dickish of me but I didn’t want someone to saying anything bad about my father.


“Oh? Who says I haven’t lost anything?”


I looked at the man, surprised at his words.


“My son, Rikuo’s father….was killed by other youkai too.”


My mouth opened in surprise but I couldn’t say anything.


“But that’s a risk you carry when you take such a position. You cannot step on the battlefield and expect only to kill others without being killed by others. My son knew this….And so did your father….and trust me, if he was as caring as you think of him, he would absolutely not want you to risk your life to go after such people.”


….


….


….


….


I looked towards the sky, at the moon as the scenes of the past appeared in front of my eyes.


“The only ones who should kill are those who are prepared to be killed.” 


The old man nodded and continued.


“Hagoromo Gitsune was someone who actually loved human beings.”


I was surprised by the sudden change of topic but didn’t say anything.


“She was married to a human and even had a son with him. In fact, I think you might have heard the name of her son. His name was….Abe no Seimei.”


“What?!” I blurted out, stunned at the reveal.


Abe no Seimei, was the foremost onmyoji of Japan, who devised many of the methods of exorcism used by the onmyoji of Japan other than the Five Principal Clans. He was a legend who gave humans a method to protect themselves against the youkai. And now this guy is telling me that the guy was half youkai himself and not just any but a Nine Tailed Fox Youkai.


“Surprising isn’t it? You see, Hagoromo Gitsune originally wanted a world where humans and youkai could coexist peacefully.”


“That’s a good but foolish dream.”


“Oh, you don’t think that’s possible?”


“While I would love to see such a world too, it’s not realistically possible unless someone extremely powerful forces them to coexist peacefully. I mean just look at the history of human beings. Even without the awareness of other races, they fight wars with each other, for their greed and biases. They discriminate with each other based on their colour, religion and even languages, so much so that they would throw away their own blood because one of their parents is of a different country or religion? Do you really think such people would just put aside their biases and get along happily with beings who are far stronger than them? If you do then you must be the biggest fool in the world. I know there are good humans in the world too but expecting so much from them is foolishness.”


The man was taken aback by the bite in my words.


“Humans always fear what they cannot understand and their first reaction against such things….is violence. I don’t know much about youkai to say anything about them but you cannot clap with one hand.”


“Well well, looks like Rikuo needs to catch up to you in more than just strength. What you said is true. That is one of the reasons for the secrecy of the Supernatural World from common humans. Anyways, Hagoromo Gitsune’s wishes were appreciated by her son who started working in the court of the emperor to try to slowly make the people more accepting of the Youkai. All that was until she was killed by a court noble of the emperor’s court.”


“Huh?”


“That fool had heard that the liver of a Shinoda fox could grant eternal youth and had sent his armies to hunt her. They brought her body to an onmyoji to prepare the elixir and guess who the guy was?” He suddenly looked at me.


My eyes went wide in realisation.


“Yes, they brought her body….to her own son. It’s safe to say that Seimei went berserk and slaughtered all the court members and left with his mother’s body. From then on, he came to be known as Nue.”


“But if they both died, then….”


“Ah yes, you don’t know much about her. You see, while Hagoromo Gitsune did die, she could reincarnate at times and possess the bodies of other people. The thing is, Abe no Seimei had devised a method of perfect reincarnation and to do so, he needed to be reborn from the same womb which had given him birth once. Hagoromo Gitsune had obviously agreed to this request of her son. Eight hundred years ago, she reincarnated as a female samurai you might have heard or. She was called Tomoe Gozen.”


Damn….another big name.


“Four hundred years ago, she had reincarnated in the courtesan Lady Yodo, she was the mother of Hideyori Toyotomi and the master of Osaka Castle. At the time Hagoromo Gitsune frequently met with her yōkai underlings in secret late at night, where she would ingest the livers they had collected for her - all for the sake of giving birth to the Nue. However the livers of average people were not good enough for the task so she switched to the hearts of people who had unusual abilities like a princess whose tears became pearls, etc. One such princess was Yoihime….my wife.”


“Huh?”


“Yeah….I fought against Hagoromo Gitsune because she was targeting the woman I had fallen in love with.”


“Your wife was a human?” I  was surprised again.


“Well yeah, how else would Rikuo be a quarter youkai?”


“I….didn’t know what percentage youkai he was, just that he was part youkai.”


“You need to learn Senjutsu soon.”


“.....hmm.”


“Anyways, I was lucky in that fight as I was assisted by Hidemoto Keikain, the ancestor of your former classmate on that day and even then, I lost my heart, leaving me permanently weakened and in this degrading self from then.”


“Your heart?”


“Yes, quite literally. She ripped it out. Youkai bodies work differently than normal human bodies. If you want to kill one, make sure they are burned completely and even ashes are scattered.”


He was weakened from that fight so….this was his weakened self and Hagoromo Gitsune and possibly Nue is stronger than him.


No….I will not let that discourage me.


“As you see, the fight you are entering is something that has continued for over a thousand years and believe it or not….it was not her who did it.”


“Why are you telling me all this?”


“So that whatever decision you make, you do so with full knowledge. You have a chance, a rare chance to get out of all this mess and start anew. Do you want to risk it all for revenge?”


….


….


“Sigh….take your time but think about all this before you enter the fray. I understand the pain of losing someone close to yourself but don’t let it become the sole guiding force in your life. That kind of life….is not pleasant. As for Senjutsu, I could guide you for a bit until you are competent enough to hide your aura. I am not exactly a master of the art myself to teach you much further.”


“....why are you doing all this for me?”


“Hmm? You are Rikuo’s friend, aren’t you? And you will have to pay me with Kofuko Manju. I am not teaching you anything for free. Actually, you know what?...I have another plan in mind.”


“What plan?” I asked, cautiously.


He told me his plan and by the end of it, I was looking at him incredulously.


“Are you sure that will work?” I asked.


“Well, it’s worth a try. Just do it for me, please.”


I nodded unsurely.


“....Thank you for everything, Nurarihyon.”


We just sat there without any words as I thought about what he had said then.


Grandma had also said something similar.


To let go.


But….every time I tried thinking of it, the scene of my father's body appeared in front of my eyes and all that thought went to hell.


- Nurarihyon -


He returned back to his Clan early the next morning after guiding the boy with the basics of Senjutsu.


While that was nowhere near enough to learn Senjutsu, it wasn’t someone one learned in a day, who knew with that kid.


His arm still stung from last night’s attack.


Just how the hell did that kid train all this time?


If anyone heard that the infamous Supreme Commander of the Nura Clan got injured by a fourteen year old, he would become a joke for the whole world.


Seriously, he needs to kick Rikuo’s ass to get him to train.


As he reached the gates of his clan, several youkai rushed forward.


“Supreme Commander, are you okay? Did that man attack you? Why is your kimono burnt?” Karasu Tengu was the one in the lead as usual.


“Hmm, this…. is nothing. I was doing a barbeque and didn’t watch out.”


….


They all stared at him and soon Rikuo also came running out.


“Grandpa….wait, are you hurt?”


“No….this is nothing. Let’s go inside and talk. I am tired.”


Sitting in his room, his daughter-in-law brought him a cup of tea while Rikuo and the rest of his group sat in front of them. Surprisingly, the mermaid was also with them.


“Thank you, Wakana. Now, you all seem to have something to talk to me about.”


“Umm….that Shadow….was he strong?” Rikuo asked after some hesitation.


“Didn’t you get beaten by him? What does that tell you?” He asked, making their faces blanche.


“B-b-but Rikuo-sama would have won if he hadn’t used that trick.” Tsurara tried to complain but one look from him shut her up.


“No….no he wouldn’t.” He shook his head.


“Supreme Commander?”


“First of all, he was using illusions, my speciality….against my descendant and won.” His words made them wither. “Second, no enemy would tell you and attack.”


“If Shadow had truly attacked, none of you would have stood a chance against him. He could have taken you all on at the same time….and won. The illusion….was probably the most harmless of his means which he used against Rikuo. Give him a year or two and he might just reach my level.” He said, before taking a sip from his cup.


All of them became quiet.


“Now, what did you all want to talk about?”


Rikuo snapped out of his thoughts first. “I….well, the boy Miss Sun is searching for….it’s my friend.”


“Your friend?”


“Yes. Tatsuya. The white haired guy whom you met before. He is one of my closest friends. Her father wants to kill him. Grandpa, what should we do?”


….


It was only the years of experience that helped prevent him from bursting out into a fit of laughter.


Oh, this was going to be fun.

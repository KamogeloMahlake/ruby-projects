“And what exactly can you provide us with? You cannot save our Clan from extinction.” She asked.


“And why exactly can We not do that?” I asked.


“You are barely a jōnin and these subordinates are chunin level or tokubetsu jōnin level at best. Even I can take you all out without breaking a sweat.” She answered with a roll of her eyes.


This made me laugh.


This woman really didn’t hold back her thoughts.


“Why do shinobi always think of fighting and killing as a solution to every problem?” I shook my head.


“It’s what they are trained to do.” She replied without any hesitation.


“Yes, We know. Honestly, it’s quite a waste of their skills. There are so many useful ways a shinobi can use their skills and yet they choose to use it for violence of all things. Water Style could be used to gather water from the air to water crops, Earth Style can be used to pave roads through the mountains and construction and yet they use it to drop and bury people. Sigh, such a massive waste of resources. But then again, it’s not the fault of the shinobi alone but also of their daimyos for only using them for such things. Anyways, let’s not get into this line of talk or We will continue to babble for the rest of the night.”


I shook my head and concentrated on the topic at hand. “There is a simple way to solve this problem. All the Uzumaki Clan needs to do is relocate to somewhere they cannot find you.”


“It won’t work. Do you truly think it’s that easy? They will search the entire Elemental Nations if we were to disappear suddenly. Since they have decided to exterminate us, they will not give up that easily.”


“Again. If they will search for you throughout the elemental nations, then why don’t you go away from the Elemental Nations?” I asked.


“Eh?”


“The world is far bigger than you think, Uzukage-sama. The Elemental Nations are not even a tenth of the whole world. There are other continents other than the Elemental Nations, some big, some small. One of them is one and a half months away from us by the ocean if you know the way. None of the people from the Elemental Nations will be able to find you there.” I finished and saw their eyes widen in surprise.


Initially, I had been planning to get some of their people for the Land of Flowers but something changed in between.


Toji asked me for a map of the world to know more about the places and that’s when I realised that I knew nothing of the world apart from the Elemental Nations so I asked my Grimoire for a map of the world and the information only took three days to get.


And that’s when I realised just how little we know of this world because no one knew of the continent I was talking about in Elemental Nations.


“You are not joking.”


“We do not joke when so many lives are at stake, Uzukage-sama. Our sense of humour is far better than that.”


“If your information is indeed correct, then the Uzumaki Clan will be the eternal allies of the Land of Flowers and the Katsuyoshi Clan. I know that you are interested in Fuinjutsu and are even teaching your people so we will even send three of our top Fuinjutsu Masters along with the ones already here to your court to teach you and anyone you wish our art, even the Fuinjutsu of the Uzumaki Clan. They will be yours to command. We can even establish an alliance in a similar way as the Senju Clan, through marriage. I know you already have a fiance so we can send you a concubine in secret to seal the deal.” She finished in one breath.


This time, it was my turn to be taken aback.


Damn, that… was a lot.


I collected myself after a few seconds and spoke. “We appreciate your offer but We would like to make some changes to the offer.”


She nodded.


“We appreciate you sending your people to our command and We won’t lie that it’s an incredible offer, swaying even us, We do not wish to take advantage of you. As We have said, We want a friendship similar to what your clan has with the Senju Clan. We do not want to separate those people from their families. If anything, you can send them to teach our people but allow them to leave when our Land has at least three Masters of our own. Even teaching them the Uzumaki Fuinjutsu will be left to your wishes and will not be forced by us. As for the offer of a concubine, We accept it but would like for it to be a year or so later. We are scheduled to marry Princess Kasumi and taking a concubine just after will not only be disrespectful to her but to the concubine as well. We cannot and will not do that.”


She just stared at me for a while and then opened her mouth again. “You are a fool, you know that, right?”


“Hahahaha, yes. We have been called that by our retainers several times.” I laughed before taking out a piece of paper from my sleeve pocket and handing it to her. “This is the map to the continent.”


She quickly took the paper and looked at the map, memorising it with almost a look and then keeping it in her sleeves.


“Thank you. I will meet you again after verifying this information and discuss further plans. Again, thank you.” She replied with a bow.


“Don’t worry. This is something We would have done regardless. After all, two of our teachers are from the Uzumaki Clan. We cannot sit aside and see them perish.” I smiled.


This time even she smiled. “You really are a fool.”


“We have a feeling that you prefer this version.” I smiled and replied.


“You do realise that I could finish you off right now and take this map away and no one would ever know where the Uzumaki Clan goes? If anything, you become more of a liability after giving us this information than an ally.”


I smiled at her words.


“Obviously I know that… but tell me, do you seriously think We would walk in without any preparations?” I asked.


“With just you five? There are only you and your retainers here and no other humans in a five kilometre radius of us and I could take you all on and leave without sweating.” She replied.


“Oh, and how strong does Uzukage-sama think we are?”


“I have heard that you have some technique to hide your chakra but your chakra was sensed when you were training. You are a jōnin level at best, an average jōnin.”


“Hoh.” I looked at Genshiro- sensei and released my chakra blocking technique and let my chakra run wild. I saw his eyes widen. “Was this what you sensed last time? The reason you sensed our chakra was not because you caught us off guard but rather because we wanted you to sense us.”


Even the Uzukage was slightly surprised. “Alright, definitely more than I expected. An Elite Jōnin level at such an age was not something I was expecting. Still, it’s not something I cannot manage.”


“Obviously, we know that. But tell me, Uzukage-sama. Have you perhaps heard of the Eight Inner Gates?”


“The Eight Gates are eight specific tenketsu along the chakra pathway system which  exist in order to limit the flow of chakra within an individual's body.”


“Exactly. Normally, individuals can use no more than 20% of their body's full potential, the brain's way of protecting the individual from harming their body through overexertion. With training, individuals can learn to remove these limiters, referred to as "opening" the gates. With each opened gate, individuals are given access to more and more of their body's chakra, thus increasing their physical strength and speed. If one can open all eight gates, they can gain power surpassing even the likes of Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha but the only catch is that you will…”


“Die.” She completed. “Yes. I am aware and so are many others and yet in thousands of years, no one has been able to devise a method to open these gates safely…” And then she paused looking at me with a slightly open mouth. “You did not?”


“But of course We did.” I looked at Kuina and ordered. “Would you please give us a demonstration? Not much just opening till the third gate will be enough.”


She nodded and walked to a distance from us and took a deep breath and suddenly her chakra roared.


She went from Tokubetsu Jōnin level to almost Elite Jōnin levels, her chakra cracking the ground under her as the wind from her chakra release blew away the trees around us. She drew her sword and the next moment, her figure blurred and appeared near a rock, sending a wave of chakra which cut apart dozens of trees.


“That will be enough.” I ordered and she reverted back to her normal state again while I looked at the stunned Uzukage and spoke calmly. “While We might not be able to rival Uzukage-sama in terms of experience, skills or even chakra.” I narrowed my eyes. “We can make sure that if our life is truly endangered, We drag our enemies down together with us to become nourishment for the very flowers our land gets its name from.”


I was obviously not serious. I couldn’t do shit to her if I didn’t open at least six or seven gates, something I didn’t have the ability to do right now.


We stared at each other and I was thanking all that was good that I had done the previous Mind Ritual before meeting her or I would have obviously shown some flaw.


She backed down after a few moments and I smiled and continued.


“Obviously, as We have said. We are not exactly into violence so We have also prepared some backups. For example, some of our retainers have also been given this same map and told of our plan. They know of our meeting and if anything were to happen to us… well, you can guess that.”


“Good. If you want to survive on this continent, you would need to have at least this kind of preparation. Your attitude reminded me of Hashirama Senju’s but you do not have the strength like him to back it up. If you continued like that, you would just get taken advantage of and die before you can reach your full potential. That would be an incredible shame.”


“We thank you for your advice.”


The rest of the meeting went on without any problems.


Genshiro-sensei had told them about my odd hobby of collecting chakra beasts and other things and as the leader of a Shinobi Village, she had far more information and even access to these things.


We were promised these things and in return we sell them food and gold to prepare for their relocation. In the other continent, the currency from the Elemental Nations will not work so they had to liquidise everything too.


I even asked them for the prisoners they had, citing targets for testing out new Medical Ninjutsu we were developing as a reason.


She didn’t even blink an eye at the demand.


Prisoners of war were treated as dead in this world unless they were some big shots in which case, they would likely actually die rather than get captured.


After all, there is a thing called the Bingo Book as well as a bounty on the heads of any decent ninja around.


We parted ways after discussing the basics and decided to finalise things after they verified my information.


I slumped on my couch after reaching home.


“That was a dangerous game you played out there, my Lord.” Saitō said as he took a seat too.


“And yet We couldn’t back down at such a time.”


“She could have attacked you.” Kuina said, sweat visible on her forehead.


“No. There was no way she was going to attack us. You don’t get to be the head of a ninja village by being an idiot. If she truly wanted to attack us, she wouldn’t have said anything and returned and verified the news and then have us assassinated. Worst case, they could have tried to use some Fuinjutsu to control us. In both cases, I had ways to escape and then ruin them. Moreover, they didn’t know if we had any back ups.” Worst case, I would have taken me and all my subordinates inside the Grimoire and set the time to return back after ten days.


It was a risky move no doubt but I am pretty sure we could have left if we wanted to. They couldn’t afford to make overt moves here and attract attention.


It’s going to take them at least two months to verify the news and I would have enough time by then not only to prepare for any eventualities but also get Rōen and secure a method to fight against them in worst case.

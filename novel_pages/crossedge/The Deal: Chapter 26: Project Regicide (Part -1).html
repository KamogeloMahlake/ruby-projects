Sona Sitri was taken hostage to keep Serafall Leviathan and other Satans from making any moves… but she was also under the protection of Charlene and Helena and coincidentally all the cameras in the place were active to allow them to keep an eye on the entire situation to keep them from doing anything rash.


They had even placed a couple of their supporting House Heads among the crowd who fanned the flames against Zekram during the meeting with the Satans, making them take action.


Gretel sat in front of the screen and watched the proceedings.


The three attacking teams entered the Bael estate and immediately encountered resistance in the form of the Peerage members of the Bael Head. However, they were easily taken care of by the attackers, especially the kings who were boosted by the King Piece.


"There is a variable in the plan." A message came to her from the other side.


"Yes?"


"Venelana Gremory is visiting the Bael House for some reason." Cleria relayed the situation.


Her?


But there was no time to change the plans right now.


“Try not to harm her.”


“Okay. I will inform Alicia, Victoria and Ophelia to take care.”


She too got to work as both sides got busy fighting.


Grey Mist spread out from her hands and seeped into the ground under the Bael Territory, directly under the palace of the Great King.


She could feel everything through her connection to her Sacred Gear. The grey mist was moulded into a dimensional gateway and connected to a separate dimension they had already connected beforehand.


The gateway was then connected to the basement of the Bael Estate.


With all the preparations complete, she threw one last look at the place and teleported away.


- Zekram Bael -


He watched as everything was going wrong and yet several things didn’t make sense.


Those rebels.


He could barely understand the two who were told to return their King Pieces rebelling. After all, they were given the option to pass on the pieces to their children and keep the influence of their houses.


Was their pride or attachment to their power so great that they would ruin everything, even the reputation of their houses and its futures for that?


But even that had a reason.


Still, for the life of him, he couldn’t understand why the other four rebelled all of a sudden.


Were they afraid that they would be sidelined as well?


No.


Something of this level of planning couldn’t be done in a few months, let alone a few days. This was something which had been planned for a long time.


But who was behind them? As far as he knew them, none of them had what it took to plan all this.


So who could it be?


The current Satans?


No.


They might make moves but this was not their style.


His butler’s and informants of the Old Satan faction were revealed so maybe… those Satan Descendants?


Then again, it’s not like there weren’t some other suspicious people who could do it too.


Diehauser Belial.


Cleria Belial’s case was dangerous because of the people associated with her.


It could be his political rivals.


Agares.


But then again, with the number of spies he had with everyone, it was very unlikely that they could pull something like this off without alerting him one way or another.

Or was it other factions?


No. That was very unlikely.


He never put himself in front of other factions.


The new Satans were there for that.


The only viable option he could think of is Rizevim.


Maybe… but then why would he make a move all of a sudden?

And these brats were being guided so easily as well.


Just as he was thinking about these, the scene on the screen shifted to a familiar place.


The Bael Mansion?


Similar barriers rose around the entire mansion as several Devils rushed in to take over the mansion.


The guards of the place reacted as expected but against the King Piece users like Beedeze Abaddon and Charles Shax, they proved to be quite short.


It would have been far different if he had been there personally. He possessed two King Pieces at hand for such emergencies to defend the Bael House.


That said, with him in this place, it was not at all possible.


He looked at the Satans.


“I request assistance for the Bael House. Whatever may be the case, can still be left till everything is proved but if the Bael House dies, it will be all on you people.”


Several people cried out in outrage at this.


He noticed that it was mostly the newly instated Pillars who were making the noise.


The reason why he had let them be instated was because they couldn’t have caused any waves. The entire economy of the Underworld was already in his grasp. He could easily turn them against those brats and teach them their place.


But…


“A few minutes ago, Lord Bael requested assistance and we have already sent people to act as reinforcements. Unfortunately the barriers they are using are not something we have been able to crack. Even Ajuka would need at least two days to crack something like this so I apologise if we cannot support them faster.” Sirzechs answered. “For now, we can only wait until we are able to crack their barriers.”


He clenched his fists but kept quiet due to the situation.


The scene continued and the only ones holding the attack were the current Lord and surprisingly Venelana. It was then he remembered that he had called her to discuss the position of the Gremory and leave a warning.


He didn’t know that she had gone there today.


This ought to bring Sirzechs in line.


As expected, he looked visibly disturbed at the sight of Venelana.


The surprising thing was that the attackers, specifically, Charles Shax went ahead to negotiate with Venelana rather than continue fighting.


They offered to let the ones uninvolved, go if they surrendered and not harm even a single person.


“Our goal is to show the crimes of the Bael House to the world. We do not wish for unnecessary bloodshed. The hostages are only to prevent us from being taken down before we can achieve our goal.” Charles argued.


They brought the captured wife of the current head and his son and he could see Venelana struggling before finally giving up with a sigh.


Useless.


All of them, useless.


She was brought aside and soon the current head was also captured and locked together.


The attackers entered the mansion.


He wasn’t too worried about what they might find inside as most of the important things were hidden under several spells. Even the safes were under several seals which would destroy the contents if opened by anyone other than him.


And sure enough, one of them found one of his safes.


The next scene however completely blew his mind.


Two women, the bishops of Charles Shax took charge of the situation and one of them easily unlocked the safe. The entire sealing formation fell apart.


The other woman closed her eyes and stood in the middle of the room as she turned around, directly walking towards the next safe.


That was…


A Valac?


It was exactly how a Valac uses their Seeker’s Eyes.


He looked around and sure enough several others had recognised it as well. The head of the Valac house was also looking at the woman.


“Lord Valac… I might be wrong but that was your Clan’s Trait. But I didn’t know someone of your clan had joined Charles Shax’s peerage.”


“I… don’t know.” The man himself looked genuinely surprised.


“There is no user of the Seeker’s Eye who is as proficient as that woman on the screen. Those safes were hidden under several spells and even for someone with Seeker’s Eyes, it must be extremely difficult to pinpoint something like that. If it was actually Seeker’s Eye, then she might be the strongest user of the Skill yet.” Ajuka said and while he too agreed with it too.


Charles Shax… was he hiding such a piece from everyone?


Seeker’s Eye was probably the most useful Skill to have by anyone’s side. A reason why anyone carrying that trait was highly wanted by everyone.


He too had tried to arrange a marriage between one of his descendants a few generations ago with a Valac but unfortunately, the man died in the Civil War without producing any descendants.


To think another one was hiding right under his nose…


But at this time, she was proving to be the biggest nemesis of his as the woman found each and every one of his safes one after another.


The bigger surprise however was the second woman who was able to open them all without making a single mistake.


One by one all the safes he could recall were found and opened.


Those traitors made copies of the documents and broadcasted everything.


It was then that he noticed something odd.


Some of the things broadcasted were not things he had kept there.


They were framing him for different things.


But before he could point it out, he was attacked by someone.


He barely defended but another attack threw him aside.


The attackers were quickly apprehended and he was secured but also bound.


“Sirzechs, what is the meaning of this?”


“You will be under arrest until all the documents are verified. If even a quarter of the documents are true…”


“Those are not mine. I am being framed.” he shouted but was trapped inside the barrier.


Ajuka.


- Chysis Gremory -


She looked at Zekram who was banging at the barriers, claiming to have been framed.


In fact, she doubted him for several of those crimes and even knew that he had committed some of them directly or indirectly but… they had a policy of mutual non interference.


Zekram had knowledge of everyone’s hidden skeletons and that was not something anyone wanted to try testing out.


But this…


She didn’t know what had gone on between him and these rebels but she knew one thing for sure.


After today, the Bael House will be ruined.


Even now, several of their peerages were trying to calm down the public and hundreds of Rating Game players from going on riots.


Not to mention the other Pillar Houses who were plotted against or had their members killed by the Great King faction.


Was it really Rizevim making a move?


If so… then she could only pray to the Satans to save the Underworld.


She watched the screen with bated breath, wondering what would come out next.


The Valac woman found another direction but this time there were no safes or hidden cases.


It was a door or more precisely, a gateway.


The other woman tried to unlock it but this one took her quite long but when it was opened, it revealed a passageway to what looked like a separate dimension.


Charles Shax walked in, followed by the two women and the audience all saw the scene.


It looked like a lab.


Equipment filled every corner of the place.


They looked around and suddenly… the one recording everything stopped and several gasps sounded from the group.


“That’s…”


The camera fell from his hand and lay on the ground for a moment before it was picked up with shaking hands and pointed at a glass cylinder…


In there lay a figure.


As her brain registered what she was seeing… her mind went blank and she forgot to breathe.


The crowd around her as well as the Satans all stood up from their places and even Zekram became dead still in shock at the sight of the figure.


“It couldn’t be…”


“But how…”


At this time, she felt a tug at her sleeves and looked at Rias who was looking at the scene curiously.


“Who is she?”

“Lilith.”

- Tatsuya -


I returned to the hotel I was staying at late in the morning. My Shadow Clone was still there, keeping a guard.


I dispelled it when I entered the room and laid on the bed.


The result of the fight was as I had expected after all, I wasn’t expecting to defeat the Lord of the youkai with just a year or two of training. It was the process which surprised me.


He won with an illusion magic… no, yojutsu.


The man had explained the difference between the different types of powers used by the Youkai. Nurarihyon’s illusion was his personal, unique fear or the yojutsu which runs in his bloodline.


So many different styles of magic and each having their own advantages and weaknesses. It just goes to show how vast this world truly is.


Inside the house, I entered the Training Dimension.


I was not in the mood to summon Shanks. That guy would keep on teasing me the entire time. Same for Tobirama, who would keep on scolding me.


Itachi might not look like it but he was pretty savage in his comments too.


Hmm, Hashirama it is.


I summoned him and told him about the training and spar with Nurarihyon. Even the things he told me about the Senjutsu of this world.


“Oh, is there such a thing? I didn’t know that was possible. Manipulating Nature’s Energy directly to do things sounds fantastic. I can think of several things which would give it an advantage over our Senjutsu style. At the same time, there are things our Senjutsu style can have an advantage in.”


“Like?”


“Hmm, since you directly manipulate Nature's Energy, you don’t have to go through the complex process of taking in Nature's Energy, mixing it in equal proportion of Physical Energy and Spiritual Energy to make Senjutsu Chakra, thus eliminating the dangers associated with it. Even the time needed will be reduced depending on your mastery of the skill. Finally, you won’t be limited to ten minutes of Sage Mode since it wouldn’t just end with you using up all of your Senjuutsu Chakra.” He said after some thought. “This way, you can use the power for as long as you are not down.”


And the disadvantages?


“Let’s see. I would have to see it in use to know exactly but I would say, you would need a strong body and affinity with Nature’s Energy to manipulate Nature’s Energy to that extent. Second, it could be weaker at initial stages compared to Sage Mode since you get added Physical Energy and Spiritual Energy on top of the Nature’s Energy. But as you master the ability more, you can manipulate more Nature’s Energy, making up for the Physical Energy and Spiritual Energy. You couldn’t boost your jutsu with this variant of Nature’s Energy as you would need it to be assimilated with your chakra.” He added after thinking a bit more.


I looked at the man in amazement. He derived all that from the basic information I had told him.


The title God of Ninja wasn’t all due to the Asura’s Chakra maybe? The man was incredibly talented on his own.


Apparently he took my silence as a sign of me being depressed and quickly started reassuring me. “But all these are just my guesses. I don’t know much about the Senjutsu of that world so I can’t say much with certainty. You should take up that person’s offer and have him teach you the Senjutsu of your world. Learning new things is always fun.”


I nodded. “Actually, do you think I am ready to learn Senjutsu? You know the type you use?”


I still hadn’t reached the point where I could learn it from the Toad Sages and the Snake Sages weren’t even shown in the Shippuden Manga so I couldn’t summon them either. That only left Jiraiya, someone who didn’t even know how to use Senjutsu perfectly himself and… Hashirama.


Obviously, there were the Toad Sage and Hagoromo himself towards the end but that’s a long time. Actually, there was also Katsuyu but I didn’t know if she knew Sage Mode. 


“Oh, you want to learn Senjutsu? Hmm, why not. Although my own version of Sage Mode doesn’t have any special techniques compared to the Sage Modes of the other two Sage Lands that said, it has the most amount of raw power. It’s just that… to learn my version of Sage Mode, you need to learn Wood Style first.”


“Wood Style?”


“Hmm. Learning Wood Style would allow you to understand Nature more, understand how the energy flows in nature. You will then need to guide the energy into your body.”


“I see. I thought your Sage Mode probably came from the Land of Slugs.”


“The Land of Slugs? No, not really. Why would you think so?” He asked, confused.


“Umm, because Tsunade uses the Slug Summoning Contract and they are known as one of the three Holy Sage Lands.”


“Oh, Tsuna got a summoning contract from the Slugs? They are good summoning beasts but no, the Senju Clan didn’t have that contract. I guess she must have found it herself. Good for her.”


“I see.”


That cleared up the confusion I had for a long time… and also made me realise just how much of a monster Hashirama Senju was all over again.


With that, I started working on Wood Style. As Hashirama said, it helped me sense Nature’s Energy, something which would help me learn both world’s versions of Senjustu easily.

Sea King Meat from the One Piece world.


It increases the strength of the body passively and fulfils the demand of energy for the intensive training.

“Hello, am I speaking to Akii Tori-san?”

“This is good. But how long did it take you to make?”

She quickly took it and read it carefully.


“This is… it’s almost ready to be published.”


“Hmm.”

“Yes. It’s a monthly comic and even if I get busy with my studies, I would have enough time to draw. It works perfectly with my current schedule. I just have a few requests.” I said after some hesitation.

“I see. Very well. We can make a contract when you want and publish the pilot chapter for next month’s edition.”

We talked about the details about the contract. The pay was obviously lower than Shueisha, much lower but it was also a new publishing house. It was already a lot that they were paying me this much even from their negligible income.


She then went on to introduce me to the rest of the members of the team.


Hana Sasayama, the girl who had asked me to buy the comic before reading… or well, begged me to, was the Editor-in-Chief of the company.


She was… well, enthusiastic to say the least.


Next were Tsumugi Hararaki and Ringo Nishijima, both of whom were editors. The former was… a glutton. Ringo-san was however more mature and knew her work.


I was however unbothered with them. I didn’t need an editor.


After leaving the place, I called Shuri-san and told her about the contract. She was the one who had helped me make a deal with Shueisha and after grandma, she was my guardian.


She obviously agreed to visit me and help me make a contract.

There were however disadvantages too.


First was that I need to concentrate on them to make them perform to their full potential. If I was preoccupied, their performance fell. This is also why they couldn’t be used for training to the extent Shadow Clones could be.


Shadow Clones didn’t need this concentration and directly fed me the knowledge and experience of the activities. It was hard on the mind for sure, but the experience was detailed unlike the selective things which Wood Clone could send.


If one had to compare, Wood Clones would be like dividing the mind into several parts to do some work while Shadow Clones were like injections of information.


I personally preferred using Wood Clones to draw manga, something which increased my efficiency even more. Shadow Clone was used more for repetitive training like jutsu training, elemental chakra training, etc.


“You just need to keep practising at the same rate. The Sharingan only helps so much. The Sharingan has many restrictions which were not explained in the manga. We only knew that it couldn’t copy Kekkei Genkai jutsu, Shape Transformation techniques like Rasengan which required more practice but there were other restrictions too.


For example, the Water Dragon Bullet Jutsu. It needed forty four hand seals. The Sahringan could copy it, sure… but the more someone has mastered a jutsu, the fewer hand seals they need to use it. Tobirama could do that with a single hand seal.


This… was not something the Sharingan could copy.


If the user tried it, not only would it fail, it also had a chance of injuring the user.


Sometimes I think that the reason Tobirama masters all his jutsu is not to have an advantage in battle but to counter Uchiha alone…


Every jutsu he uses, he does it in one or two hand seals… he absolutely hated it if I used the Sharingan against him.


Hashirama on the other hand was far more chill. He actually asked me to observe the jutsu with the Sharingan so that I could know what was actually happening. Then he drilled me to master it. The guy was actually happier to see both the powers used so well conjugation.


This way was faster too.

Was I just being paranoid?


Did she come here just to study and learn about humans?


If so then why did she not go to Rikuo’s school?


She would have been much safer there, with other Youkai, in Nurarihyon’s territory.


Instead, she was here.


There was no way it wasn’t related to me.


At the end of it all, there wasn’t anything I could do about the situation so I let it be.


I left the Training Room and got ready for the day. Today Koji-san was going to come with some news.


In the past four months, I have been using Wood Clones to make Manga. Due to this I was almost done with Kaichou wa Maid-sama. Only the last chapter was left today and I intended to finish it by the end of today.


I was hoping to free the slot and get a new manga, something which can help me increase my magic stat. That’s one stat which has been largely ignored.


While I practised Buddhist Magic whenever I had time, the rate of increase wasn’t that high.


I finished the last chapter by afternoon.


“Tobi, help me check a chapter.” I called Tobi to read the last chapter of Kaichi wa Maid-sama to finish the quest.


“Oh, Nii-san, did you complete the chapter?” He asked, coming in.


“Yes. The last chapter is done. Give it a read and tell me if anything is wrong.”


“Alright.”


He took the copy and finished reading and I got a notification.


Two Gacha Tickets were received and one slot was freed.


“It’s great, Nii-san. I didn’t think I would like a shoujo manga one day.”


“Manga is manga. What’s wrong with Shoujo Manga?” I replied.


“Well… my classmates say that it’s girly…” He answered embarrassedly.


“Don’t listen to them. They’re idiots.”


As we were talking, the doorbell rang.


I checked and it was Akii-san.


“Welcome. Are you here for the new chapter?”


“Yes. Is it done?”


“Hmm. Please have a seat. I will get the manuscript.”


She took a seat while I got the manuscript. In the meantime, Tobi got her some tea.


“This is good. We can directly print it as always. Sigh… you really make our work too easy, Tatsuya-kun. Sometimes I feel we are not working hard enough.”


“You all have a lot on your plate as it is. Just take a break once in a while. How have the sales been going though?”


“It’s nice overall. Your manga along with Shinobu-sensei’s manga are carrying the Magazine right now. We need more good manga, at least two more of them to establish a solid foothold.”


Her words gave me an idea.


“Actually…”


“Hmm?”


“Well, if you don’t mind, I have an idea.”


She nodded.


“You see, I just completed Maid-sama today. A total of eighty three chapters and one extra.” I said and saw her look at me dumbfoundedly. “I was wondering if I can submit another manga to the Magazine?”


“Another one? Are you sure you can do that?”


“Well… I have some free time so I don’t see why not. I will need some time.”


“Obviously, take as much time as you need. I will have to inspect your work first though.” She said, clearly happy. “Just understand that I will have to put it on hold if your health or the quality of the manga falls.”


“You don’t need to worry about that. I won’t let any such thing happen.”


“Okay then. I will check the new manuscript when it’s done.”


“Do you have any requirements for what kind of manga you want?” I asked.


“Hmm, right now we are mainly dealing with Shoujo Manga to appease a certain demographic. One more of those could be good. It would help us make a foothold in this genre. We can expand later.”


“Alright. I will give you a new manuscript in a week.”


A Shoujo Manga… I had one in mind.


Kimi ni Todoke, something I had already rolled would be perfect for this.


It’s one of the best stories out there, having sold millions of copies in my previous life for a very good reason.


“Good.”


She talked to me for a while and left with the manuscript while I went back to my room.


I needed to use the gacha.


Hope I get something good this time.


I spun the wheel and one of the tickets disappeared from my hand.


The wheel stopped at… Beelzebub.


That action comedy manga?


I think it had magic too but I don’t remember everything about it. I might check it later on. Let’s see the last roll.


The wheel rolled again, slowing down and stopping at… Inuyasha.


Fuck Yeah!!!


This is what I was talking about.


I had realised something very important in the past few years.


The stronger the people, the more points I get.


Even the people in the Supernatural World read manga. But my manga was not targeting them before. Demon Slayer manga was not that famous in the Supernatural World while Naruto was. This was because the Supernatural world was more interested in things they could associate with.


I wanted to make something centred around the Supernatural world to attract readers from that part.


Inuyasha was perfect for it.


I could actually have Rikuo promote it and gain readers among youkai at least.


Even the powers in the world were nice and while a lot of them required and were youkai based powers, I knew things I could use too.


Kikyo was very good at purification and healing. She also used Spiritual Powers so maybe she could help me train in that art. 


There was also the Shikon Jewel and the swords but I didn’t know if I could use those without youkai powers.


Demon Slayer has been for almost three years already. It will be over in one more year and I would get a chance to introduce one more manga. In that time, I can complete Kimi ni Todoke and even start Inuyasha.


It will be a proper long running shounen series. Now I just need to find a place to publish One Piece.

I started working on Kimi ni Todoke the very next day.


“Ikuse-senpai.” My concentration was broken by the voice of Midori Hayakawa, the vice president of the Arts Club of our school.


“Yes?”


“Umm, we have a meeting for the Arts Club.” She said, hesitating slightly.


Ah yeah…


Midori-chan was the Vice President but I… was the President of the Arts Club. These kinds of things were my responsibility.


Why did I take this position again?


Well, I was in my senior year and the rest voted me into the position.


“Okay, let’s go.” I gave her a smile and the girl cheered up instantly.


“Yes. Today we need to discuss what we are going to do for this year’s School Festival. Everyone is very excited.”


And they were indeed very excited. The entire club was abuzz with discussions.


“Taicho!” One of the guys noticed me and stood up. The rest followed.


I had a pretty great reputation in the club. This was due to many reasons.


First was me being at the top of my year. It made many of them look up to me with admiration. Second was due to Tobi, the current ace of the Kendo Club, who had somehow let it out that I was the one who taught him swordsmanship.


Since then, not only the students of the Kendo Club but also the Club Advisor had approached me several times to invite me into the club. They even had arguments with the Arts Club members who thought they were trying to poach their members.


The matter had only calmed down when I publicly denied their invitation and stated that I wanted to become a mangaka.


Last reason was… Sun Seto.


“Is everyone here?”


“Yes, Taicho.”


“Good. Then let’s start the discussions. The school festival is at the end of this month and we need to make a show from the Arts Club. Does anyone have any ideas?”


And the discussions began.


“We can do a puppet show.”


“Eh, who knows how to do that?

“How about we make an arts exhibition?”


“Ughh, we do that every year. Can’t we do something new on top of that?”


“Like what?”


“I don’t know? The Games Club did a Maid Cafe last year and it was very popular.”


“Ehh… no way. I can’t wear something like that. That’s so embarrassing.”


“Ano… how about we…”


“What is it, Midori-chan?”


“Umm… why don’t we do cosplay?”


“Hah? Cosplay?”


“Yes yes. We can totally cosplay anime characters. Many of us want to become artists or mangaka in the future. We can try to make our passions known to others and even promote manga among the students. We can do a presentation on manga as well as how the manga industry is not just some comics for kids but a large and expanding business avenue worth billions of yen.”


The room quieted down.


Manga and manga readers were looked down upon in this day and age. I knew that it would change a lot in the next decade or two but right now, these people face teasing and ridicule from others when they tell their dreams to others.


Perhaps the only reason no one told me anything was that I always aced in my studies. That said, even then, the Principal of the school had once asked me if I really wanted to be a mangaka.


“That’s… do you think it will work?” Someone asked, breaking the silence.


“Who knows… but does that mean we shouldn’t try? It’s not as if we have anything to lose. If we are going to be called otakus and ridiculed either way, then we might as well try.”


Midori-chan was usually so shy but she was extremely passionate about manga.


“Right! What do we have to fear? We can totally pull this off.” I too gave them a few words of encouragement.


This topic… it was close to my heart too.


Everyone looked at me. “Really Taicho?”


“Yeah. Go on, Midori-chan, tell us more about your plan.”


“Yes, Taicho. We can make the presentation from a technical point of view by introducing different types of manga and how manga are made, how much work is put into making even one volume of any manga. We can even make some decorations about different famous mangaka and their achievements. Don’t let anyone look down on how competitive this field is.”


“Hmm, that’s a great idea.”


“Right? Then we can explain about other fields which are related to manga like Anime, Light Novels and games, all of which come together to create a mega giant of an industry which has many opportunities for all. In fact, it would have been even better if we could invite someone from the Manga industry as a guest to explain about the subject. But in case we can't, we can do something related to our favourite manga.” She continued.


She had thought a lot about this.


“What kind of thing?” One of the members asked.


“Well… we can make artwork related to our favourite manga. In fact, I have heard from Tobio Ikuse-kun that it was Taicho who taught him the dance he did last year. He could totally pull a Tanjiro from Demon Slayer…”


“Oi, what the hell is that about me dancing?” I interrupted her in the middle.


This girl was really cutting close to home.


But the fire had already been set.


The entire club exploded.


“Really? That was you?”


“Yeah! That was actually awesome. And now that I think of it, it did kinda look like the Hinokami Kagura.”


“Right! Ikuse-senpai could totally do it better.”


“And we could also collaborate with the Music Club for this performance.”


“I am not dancing. No way.” I waved the topic off.


“But senpai!”


“Taicho, didn’t you say you wanted to be a mangaka? We are promoting manga this time. We can make our entire theme related to Manga. The art can be manga related, the dresses, even the performance can be manga related.”


“All just to make me dance? Are you crazy?”


“It’s for manga, Taicho. We are going to show all those people that Manga is art.”


“Yeah, we will prove that we are not useless otaku.”


Holy crap, just where is the conversation going?


“Taicho!”


“Please Taicho!”

“And just like that, I was surrounded by the entire Arts Club and forced into the whole thing.” I sighed.


“I think you should do it, Nii-san. Remember that you were the one who told me that I can join the dance club too. Why can’t you do the same?” Tobi said, suddenly bringing out something from the past.


“Yes yes. I think Tatsuya-san would look good too.” Sae-chan said. “Don’t you think so, Sun-senpai?”


“Eh… ah, yes.” The girl nodded.


She had started walking home with us since the start of the school year. Her reason was that she didn’t know other people… and it was a decent reason.


Sae-chan and Tobi being the helpful kind they were, welcomed her gladly.


I didn’t have any excuse to deny either. There was no reason I could leave her alone with Sae and Tobi.


“Hmm. Grandma also said that it was a very pretty dance. Please do it, Nii-san. I did it before too.”


“Aghh… alright alright. It’s not like I can back out now. Everyone has already started working on the art already.”


“I can prepare the costume for the dance if you want.” Sae-chan offered.


“No it’s fine, Sae-chan. I have a spare piece. I’ll use that.” The cost of the costume in the System was cheap. “Also, I will be using Yoriichi’s costume instead of Tanjiro’s.”


A good thing that I would be getting a mask to perform.

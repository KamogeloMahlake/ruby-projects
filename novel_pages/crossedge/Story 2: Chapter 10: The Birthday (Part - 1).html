Lord Belial took the chance to ask something. “Say Lord Glasya-Labolas, do you know anything about the new Lord Buné? From what I have seen the new Lord has a good head on his shoulders but what kind of person is he?”


“Mathias? I’ll be honest but I don’t know much about him apart from the fact that Armin respects him immensely and considers him a brother no less than Falbium. We have only had a chance to meet once during Armin’s birthday. My wife seems to know and adore him a lot though. I guess I will have to rectify this.”


“He is good friends with Sona too and Serafall seems to think well of him. From what little I have heard, he seems to be close to Euclid. I think he is quite close to the Gremory Heiress too.”


“At least he seems to have a decent political awareness. Getting close to the prominent members of the next generation.” Lord Astaroth commented. “He seems to be quite close to Latia too since Leila was his teacher.”


Yes, he was really waiting for the announcement to see his expression and as if the Satans had heard his wish, he saw Sirzechs taking the stage.


“You seem to know about him too, Lord Gremory. Would you add something?” Lord Belial looked at him then.



"Look there. You will know." He pointed towards the stage.


They turned around just in time to see Sirzechs begin.


“Ladies and Gentlemen, a good evening to you all.”


Everyone turned towards him and he continued.


“I am very pleased to be here on today’s occasion. I met Mathias, six years ago, when a twelve year old child had come to my office to receive his Evil Pieces. Fair to say I was surprised. A boy, his age coming from the Buné lands of all places. On asking further I found out that his parents, former Lady and Lord Buné had disappeared. We investigated and we all know the results. In a way, the criminals were caught due to the contribution of Mathias on that day.”


“That was a scandal, alright. To think they would be so crazy. They are lucky they were able to escape with just a lifetime imprisonment. If left outside, I doubt they would have survived past a month.” Lord Sitri commented.


“A week would be pushing it. I would give them a week at best. They crossed the line when they started experimenting on the members of other Houses.” Reven commented.


“Hmm.”


“But that was not the end of my surprises that day. On that day, I found out that Mathias possessed not just one Clan Trait but two, from each of his parents' side.”


Murmurs ran through the place at the reveal.


“One of them was the Buné House trait, a House which would have gone extinct had Mathias not inherited the power. And the other was a trait from an Extinct House, which he is the sole living member of. Fair to say that he passed both the tests, making him the first person to not only revive an Extinct House but also become the Head of two different Houses.”


At this point the entire venue was resounding with guesses.


“Zeo, what do you know? You have been awfully smug since the start.” Reven asked suspiciously.


He let out a smile and said. “Keep watching.”


Sirzechs waited till the crowd became quiet once more. “So, on this happy occasion, I would like to welcome a new member among us. Please welcome Lord Mathias Buné….Zagan, Ladies and Gentlemen.”


There was a moment of silence….


And the crowd erupted completely.


“By the Satans!” Lord Sitri exclaimed, looking shocked.


“So that’s why? No wonder, no wonder.” Lord Glasya-Labolas had a look of realisation on his face.


“Zagan….did he just say Zagan? That Zagan?” Reven asked, stunned.


“What other Zagan is there?”


“And he inherited that power. So all these food items and his farm….oh! You knew about it?”


“I had to keep it a secret. It was Mathias’ own secret to reveal after all. Only a handful of people knew about it due to security reasons.”


“Understandable. Heaven and the Fallen Angels had left no means untouched to remove them. I am pretty sure even the Old Satan faction would have left no methods unused to have them by their side. Their Life Magic is just that useful.” Lord Sitri offered.


“How proficient is the new Lord Zagan with his Clan Trait?” Reven asked him.


“Mathias has been doing nothing but training for the last seven years or so. Everything we are eating at this party was made by him personally.”


He observed Lord Astaroth from the corner of his eyes and saw him still in shock.


Sirzechs continued, attracting their attention. “As we all know, the Zagan House was hunted down by Heaven and the Fallen Angels to the extent that one of the Archangels did a suicidal attack on the first Zagan to take him down. They relentlessly targeted the Zagan House knowing its importance to the Underworld and the Devil Race….and there was a chance that they could have tried to do so if they had gotten a wind of the situation. Not to mention the hidden dangers like the former Naberius who were the cause of the death of the last Lord of the House of Buné, Mathias’ father, Lord Morton Buné.”


He let the information sink in before continuing. “We as the Satans, the leaders of our race, couldn’t allow our race to suffer such a loss and thus we decided to keep his identity hidden till he was ready to appear in front of the Underworld. In the meantime, he was given the time to study and prepare himself for the future. And finally today, I am glad to see him standing here, among us, as one of the upcoming pillars of the Devil Race.”


There was an applause from the audience.


“But the good news doesn’t just end. There is another announcement, something which is a matter of personal joy to me.” He smiled. “This morning, Mathias has accepted two proposals for engagements.” And there it was.


Reven looked towards him, already understanding where this was going.


He apologised. “I couldn’t say anything before for this reason. I am really sorry for not being able to say anything earlier." He had been feeling guilty to keep such a thing from his long time friend despite the circumstances.


Reven let out a sigh. “I understand. I did wonder why you were delaying the talks or diverting the topic whenever I broached the subject. I guess I was being a bit selfish to push despite getting the hint. When did it happen?”


The ones around him had also heard the talks and congratulated him as well.


“This morning. Mathias didn’t want to be engaged without Rias’ agreement and we didn’t want him to sign any agreement while he wasn’t of age to avoid whatever was coming later. We just gave them time to get to know each other and they decided to announce everything at today’s party.”


“Congratulations my friend.”


“Thank you.” He smiled, finally feeling a bit lighter after being able to reveal everything


Sirzechs continued.


“As of today, he is engaged to my own little sister Rias Gremory for the position of Lady Buné and… Latia Astaorth, for the position of Lady Zagan.”


He threw a look at Lord Astaorth who was by this point looking at the stage with an open mouth.


They too walked towards the stage where Mathias was currently being surrounded by members of the other Houses, trying to greet him with a sudden increase in enthusiasm.


The ones who were sent by their masters or house heads were currently busy calling their houses to inform them of the sudden change in the situation.


As they reached the stage, they heard a loud voice which suddenly made Reven hold his head, sighing loudly.


“Mathias Buné Zagan, I challenge you for the hand of Rias Gremory in marriage.”


He felt his eye twitch while Reven was massaging his head.


“Riser!” Ruval quickly went beside him to stop him from making a scene. “I apologise, Lord Buné Zagan. My brother was out of line.”


Thankfully it looked like Mathias didn’t take offence. He turned towards Riser to answer. “I refuse.”


“Afraid?” Riser taunted.


“Riser, be quiet.” Ruval held Riser’s shoulder to stop him.


“No. If it was just a challenge, I would gladly accept but this is not a simple challenge is it? You don’t seem to realise something very simple. If I accepted this challenge, it would be extremely insulting to my fiance because that would imply that I think of her as an object which anyone can come up and bet for. She is not an object. She is someone who I love and respect. And thus, I could but will not accept the challenge on principle.”


He felt a light smile come to his lips at this reply. While it was a brilliant political answer to give in such a situation, knowing the boy, he doubted he had even thought about that angle.


Still, as a father, it was heartening to see his daughter being engaged to a man who cared for her.


Riser seemed to have realised his mistake but he had already gone up and couldn’t back out without making a fool of himself so he went on. “Then I challenge you to a Rating Game to prove that I am a better match to Rias than you.”


Several people on both sides were watching the situation, probably trying to see how the new Lord handled the scene to get a read on his temperament.


“Zeo, how much chance does Riser have of winning?” Reven asked him, his eyes narrowed.


He looked at his friend and he knew from his expression that he was extremely angry and was holding back due to the occasion.


“Zero percent.” He answered honestly.


“...Good. Then let this fight happen. I will talk to Lord Buné after today’s party to clear out the matter myself.”


He looked at Reven in surprise. “I guess it’s also my own fault. Riser has gotten everything he has wanted since childhood and he has started taking everything for granted. His two wins in the Rating Games didn’t help either. I was hoping that getting engaged would make him serious which was why I was pushing so much for the engagement. But it seems I have to use another method to solve this problem. Let him have a taste of defeat and get to know how big the world is.”


“Are you sure? Wouldn’t it be too harsh?”


“Yes. Today, he made a scene in front of so many people. If it’s not corrected, it might get worse later. A harsh method is much better than him making some mistake later on which might cost him dearly later on.”


He let out a sigh. “Alright. I will talk to Mathias.”


– Mathias –


There was a small break and Zeoticus called me aside for some talks. I went there to see an unexpected face.


Lord Phenex.


“This is our first time meeting. I am Reven Phenex, Head of the Phenex House.” The guy offered a hand for a handshake and I reciprocated.


“Welcome Lord Phenex. I hope you liked the party.” I smiled. It would be foolish to blame him for a tantrum thrown by Riser.


“The party was wonderful. If anything I am here to apologise for the unseemly behaviour of my youngest son. He was out of line. I hope you will not take offence to it.”


“No no. It’s fine. I… the circumstances were not the best which led me to hide my identity. Although a bit misguided, in a way, I can understand Riser’s point of view too. If I am not wrong, he seemed to have a crush on Rias and a heartbreak can make people act emotionally. I don’t mind. No one was hurt.”


“Still, he should know better than to throw a tantrum at such an occasion.” The man looked really angry. “There was a reason I didn’t stop his foolishness though. If possible, would you listen to a request of mine?”


“Yes?” I asked, slightly confused.


“The reason I didn’t stop this challenge was because Zeo assured me that Riser had no chance of winning. I won’t lie. My son has gotten everything he has wanted since he was a child and is fairly talented too. He is even considered the second strongest of his generation and has won both his Rating Games in an overwhelming manner and this has done nothing but boost his ego too much. And all of this has led to what happened today. If this continues then sooner and later, he will do something from which I won’t be able to protect him from.” he explained.


I understood his reasons. This man was genuinely worried about his son.


“So I request you… to defeat my son in the Rating Game.”


“Huh?”


“Please defeat Riser in a Rating Game completely. In return, I promise you an alliance with the House of Phenex.” He offered.


I looked at the man, stunned to even speak anything. I then took a look at Zeoticus who nodded slightly.


“Alright. I agree.”


We shook hands to seal the deal. And this was how my first Rating Game was decided.


I wondered what else would happen tonight as we were only halfway through the party.

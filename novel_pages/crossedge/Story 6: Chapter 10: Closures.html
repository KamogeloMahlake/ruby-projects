Friendship between their group helped them live under the control of Clayman and still maintain hope and their hopes were rewarded in the form of Salvatore.


The Saviour.


His parents had indeed chosen the perfect name for him. Salvatore had come in like a storm, blowing away all their problems and then offering them shelter and a new life.


On the battlefield they thought would be their last, he had broken through their bindings and gave them freedom again.


She still remembered his fight with the Chaos Dragon on that day….


While Cleria didn’t know how strong Heavenly Dragons were, she had seen a former Dragon King like Tannin fight and….even Tannin’s strength seemed insignificant in front of that monstrous dragon.


And Sal had not only fought it but contained and purified it, stopping it from causing any harm to others.


She had heard rumours in the Underworld that Sirzechs Lucifer was ten times stronger than the previous Lucifer but she didn’t think he would last five minutes against King Salvatore in a fight, especially considering that Salvatore used Holy Magic infused Swordsmanship as his primary means.


The best part was the fact that they were from the same world and despite being brought up in a Church Orphanage with his brother for more than half his life, he was not at all biassed towards devils, even having supported her relationship. He understood their situation the best, being involved with Rimuru-sama in a relationship.


Her life had changed completely in the last month once more after coming under his rule. She was named, a ritual which increased her power to levels at which she was confident of facing her cousin in a straight fight. Even Masaomi became stronger, becoming a sage.


But perhaps the best part was the fact that she got married to the love of her life.


Cleria loved her current life.


Working for King Salvatore with her husband, teaching the kids and watching over the workings of the Kingdom and spending time  with her new friends….it was a perfect life.


That was not to say they didn’t have any worries.


King Salvatore’s memories of different worlds had been a smack on the face for both Masaomi and her.


Their world….couldn’t be that messed up right?


Could it be that Salvatore was too drunk on the day and forgot or misremembered the memories for once?


The future of their entire world lies in the hands of a….dumb teenager who retains nothing but breasts in his mind….


Was it perhaps a blessing that they were summoned to this world? A reward for remaining faithful to their love?


Because just thinking about it made her shiver with disgust.


Salvatore felt the same too, being a resident of their world too.


Talk about surprises, the wielder of Dragon King Vritra’s Sacred Gear had somehow revived the Dragon and even transformed her into a True Dragon….a True Dragon. That title was only given to one being in their world.


While Masaomi was happy to know that he was related to the church and his brother might be trained as an exorcist, the whole probable future of the world still weighed heavily on their minds too.


No wonder Salvatore was trying so hard to get back to their world.


Still, she had sworn an oath to serve him with Masaomi, loyally and she would follow through with her oaths. She didn’t want anyone to ruin her perfect life either.


That’s why when she spotted the invaders in the line of sight, she didn’t hold back.


“Five Intruders detected.”  the voice of Lord Solomon echoed in her head as the information about the intruders flooded her mind.


Her Clan Trait Worthless had evolved into an Intrinsic Skill when she was summoned to this world. It had the same function as her magic but was far more efficient in the use of it.


Apart from this, she got another Skill, a Unique Skill, Disrupter.


It had a similar function to her Worthless Skill, only in this case it could disrupt any physical attack completely.


When combined with her Worthless Ability, it gave her great defensive potential.


And finally the Extra Skill: Sage given to her after she was named.


This was the final piece of the puzzle. It gave her higher comprehension and thought acceleration thus allowing her to understand the Skills of her opponent far more easier than before.


It only took her a moment to understand all the information and lock onto the position of the intruders and with her next move, a barrier formed over the area.


This barrier was reinforced with both her Skills, Worthless and Disrupter, making it a perfect barrier for trapping anyone.


Since the mice were in the cage, let the hunt begin.


- Unnamed Blood Shadow -


He rushed through the forests of Falmuth, or was it Camelot now?


What did it matter for him?


The only thing that mattered was completing the mission. 


His life rested on it.


Sigh….


Just how did he get stuck in this mess?


One moment he was submitting a thesis for his Doctorate degree and the next moment he was in this damned world, stamped with a slave brand, forever bound to serve the ones who summoned him or die.


Those five bastards treated all the summoned otherworlders lower than dirt. Even disposable pawns would be treated better than them.


Evans was sure they must have killed at least hundred or so of them to have such kind indifference.


He hated the feeling of being treated like dirt….but there was nothing he could do.


They had the switch to his life under their thumbs and would dispose of him at the slightest inconvenience.


He had witnessed enough deaths of his fellow friends to know this.


That's why he worked hard. In the last four years, he fought tooth and nail to survive, just for a chance at survival, just in the hopes that one day things would change.


Perhaps he could become strong enough to break through these shackles.


His hard work paid off as he rose through the ranks at a high speed and even became an Enlightened, something very rare few became in this world.


It was however still not enough to free him from these chains.


As for becoming a Saint….Evans never hoped for it, realising the rarity of those in this world, the less said about a Hero, the better. That one had a lot to do with a matter of chance rather than skill of an individual. Existences such as them was far beyond his level. The likes of Hinata Sakaguchi and Salvatore, the king of the nation they were infiltrating were like stars in the sky. He could see them but never hoped to reach them.


Especially Salvatore. That was the person Evans both admired and envied.


A person in exactly the same position  as him and yet he rose beyond the crowd and became someone who could take over his captors and rule.


Even when infiltrating his Kingdom, he couldn’t help but think of the man and how he might be face to face with the same guy in this mission.


He doubted his chances of survival. Unfortunately for him, at least this way he still had some chances.


The other side was certain death.


All his thoughts were suddenly broken when he sensed the shift in mana of the area.


Looking around, he saw a semi-transparent barrier all around the place.


Shit.


“Separate and run!” He commanded in a loud voice.


His squad members in his command suddenly became aware of the situation and tried to run in different directions but were unsuccessful.


“I am afraid that’s not an option anymore.” He heard a voice in front of him as a figure entered the barrier from outside.


The man had black hair and black eyes and was holding a sword in his hand.


“I suggest that you don’t put up any resistance. If you do that, we could free you from your bindings and take you under the protection of Camelot. Trust us, we do not have any intention of fighting if you don’t.” Another voice came from behind them. On looking in that direction, he saw a man white haired man in glasses walking slowly towards them from there.


His heart stopped for a moment at those words but he quickly shook off the feeling.


There is no way that’s true. They were probably baiting him to drop guard.


He unsheathed his blade and dashed forward, intending to create a diversion with his attack. While doing this, he activated his Skill, Gemini, creating a clone of himself and joining him to flank the black haired guy.


Meanwhile, his teammates took on the while haired one.


The man defended himself with his sword, pushing him back.


What strength! However that would not be enough.


His clone was already in position to take down the neck of the guy but the next instant, something unbelievable happened.


The clone, whose sword was just on the verge of touching the black haired guy….just vanished suddenly in thin air.


“What?!” The sudden change broke his concentration and one of his steps fell in the wrong place, destabilising his balance and the black haired guy didn’t miss this opportunity and was on his face in the next instant, the next attack flinging his sword from his hand, leaving him without any weapon.


Was this the end?


Evans closed his eyes, accepting his death but the finishing blow never came.


Instead, he felt his magic being suppressed and his body being bound, leaving him unable to move.


Ah….they were probably going to be tortured for information.


Too bad for them, they were just not capable of giving out any information.


While he couldn’t move, he could hear the voices of his squadmates and see flames of a blue colour which felt….demonic.


In a matter of minutes, the whole fight was over.


His entire squad of four A Rankers and one Special A Rank was taken down in minutes without any way of resisting.


“Can you remove the binding spells on them, Cleria?” The words shook him awake from the darkness.


The next moment, he felt a hand on his forehead and a foreign power invaded his body and after four years of hell….he felt the binding on his soul….dissolve.


He opened his eyes to look at the figure in front of his eyes and saw a white haired woman sitting beside him.


She looked at him and smiled. “Don’t worry. You are free now.”


He felt a lump in his throat those words and even the years of training didn’t prevent his eyes from getting wet.


“You will be checked and if found clean, will be given a room and offered the protection of Camelot. I am sure you understand the procedures. We cannot afford spies among us.”


A tense look appeared on his face at the words. Was he just switching hands from one cruel master to another one?


“Don’t worry. You will just be asked a few questions. Nothing complicated. I suggest that you do not lie as our King can easily catch lies. Trust me, if you were forced to do this, you will not be punished. After all, we were in the same situation as you until a while ago. King Salvatore is not the kind of person who would force you into such a situation. He has even worked and banned summoning of otherworlders since becoming a King and has been working towards it since then.” The woman reassured him and he nodded.


“Alright, we are on the clock. Could you guys give us the details of your plans? That would help out a lot.”


He nodded and slowly spoke the details of the plans….or at least the part which he knew about.


Each group only knew their own route of invasion and were never told about the other groups and only the place where they would parley to act on the plan.


They were then left to the custody of a group of soldiers while the three left for their own work.


While being led to Camelot, there was only one thought in Evans’ mind. Was this his chance at a new life?

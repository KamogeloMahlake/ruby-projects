She could even hypnotise anyone using magic which worked a bit different from normal genjutsu and all this made her an incredible spy, something I had been lacking. With her around, we could do so many things that were previously out of our reach.


Finally, with her around, the Land of Flowers suddenly had a kage level, a decently strong one at that which gave us a way to protect ourselves.


Not that I was going to disclose her to the outside world.


I would prefer to have at least three to five Kage level people before disclosing one. We already saw how the Land of Sky ended. We didn’t need a repeat of that.


Kuroka had already started learning about Chakra and she was a bonafide genius at it, learning things at an incredible pace.


I can’t imagine how strong she will become in a few years, especially since she hasn’t even fully explored her original world’s powers to the fullest yet.


By the end of five years, I was pretty sure she would be someone whose name could be counted on the same line as Hashirama Senju and Madara Uchiha and then… the Land of Flowers wouldn’t have to fear anyone anymore.


And Shirone can get to the same level too… yes, the summoning with Rōen was the best thing I had done.


Although, it took me almost a week to completely recover from the targeted summoning of Shirone. According to Kuroka, it wasn’t a problem of body or mind but rather of the soul. Even with my already strong soul, I had strained myself in that ritual.


Looks like I have to do the next soul ritual sooner than I was expecting.


It required three Demon or similar beings.


The Uzumaki knew the locations for some of them, having been the ones to have sealed them. I was planning to ask the Uzukage about the location of some of them when she next comes to meet.


“Get up and start running!” My thoughts were broken by Rose’s voice who had just kicked a straggler who was actually healed of his fatigue and ran again.


“Rose-san is… quite strict.” Kanae sweatdropped at the sight too.


“Nah, I think she is perfect. I would have made them run without healing them.” Toji added and no one there doubted that the guy would do that… because he already did.


“The more injuries they suffer now, the less injured they will be on the field. I think Rose-dono’s training methods, while harsh… are actually the best we can provide these students.” Saitō added, surprising even Toji who threw a smirk at the man which the guy expertly ignored.


Rose got along surprisingly well with Saitō, both having been soldiers, commanding officers in their respective countries.


They might have different methods of teaching but they respected each other’s capabilities and treated each other politely.


Did that leave Toji as the only loner in our group?


Not at all.


“Myaah~” Shirone, in her cat form purred in Toji’s arms as he scratched behind her ears.


No one had expected that man of all people to take a liking to Shirone or the girl to like the guy either.


His reason was that he was a cat person. Actually, he treated Shirone quite well, like a rowdy uncle of some sort.


The man had actually mellowed a lot.


I think getting the ability to use chakra had done wonders about his inferiority complex over not having Cursed Energy and the treatment of the Zen’in Clan.


Now he had a power, far more versatile than Cursed Energy and he had thrown himself into exploring every intricacy of this power.


The guy had recently reached the level where he could open the sixth gate, taking the lead in our group.


The two sisters were getting along with everyone in the group quite well and surprisingly, both got along with Toji.


In fact, she had brought news that had shocked Saitō. The news about Okita Souji, one of his closest friends being the Knight of Sirzechs Lucifer and the strongest Knight of the Underworld.


The man had complex feelings about the whole thing. On one hand, he knew that it was not the same guy as the one he knew but at the same time, he was happy that he was able to survive his illness.


It however only served to motivate the man to train even harder. I guess there was some healthy competition between the members of the Shinsengumi.


Kuroka was a bit wary of everyone at the start but was slowly opening up after the girls accompanying her.


According to Kanae, she was deeply touched when she saw me pushing myself so far to summon Shirone and that’s why she could sit like she did, in her cat form, in my arms while we watched the training of the new batch of students. In fact, it was her who had helped me heal faster with her Senjutsu, which involved her sleeping with me.


The girl did so… in her cat form.


Well, at least she wasn’t treating me like her previous master so that was a plus. We can get her to warm up more later on.

“What does the letter say?” Her husband asked as she placed the letter aside.

“The eighth team has confirmed the location of another continent, one almost as big as the elemental nations. They have also discovered that the people on that continent don’t even know of the existence of Chakra so there are no dangers to our Clan on those lands. They were even able to locate a place suitable for us to set up a village.”

Most of the continent is barren but there are parts of land suitable for cultivation which makes people fight over them. Nothing different from the Elemental Nations there. There are no big countries and things are still like the Sengoku Era there.” She explained further.

“In a week. You on the other hand, start preparing for ships to begin the exodus. The injury of the Tsuchikage has led to them delaying the invasion as they don’t want to lose out on too many of their people. They are probably waiting for Onoki to heal before attacking. If they find out we are planning to move, they might leave all that caution to rest and attack us. We cannot let that happen.”

The Villager was a Tier 1 class.


It had 10 levels.


The Max Level of each Class was equal to 10*(It’s Tier).


For example a Tier Eight Class would have a max level of 80.


The Class Window was divided into ten slots, one corresponding for each Tier. The Player can Unlock any Class but they can only keep one Class active in each Tier and they will get the Stats and Skills corresponding to the active Classes. The Equipment will correspond to the Class in the Highest Active Tier active to the player.


This allowed the players to play and explore each class in the Game if they could unlock them and were willing to level them, that is, as well as enjoy completely different playstyles if they were willing to work for it. The Game Developers had worked a lot and it showed.

The piglets were easy for me to take down.

That’s the highest level apart from me in this Village.

Veena.

“Mornings are when I work for Madam Martha as her Gardener and it is not really suitable for levelling up. After all, potions were more or less the staple of all Gamers.

“Hello there. I'm Harmund. You need to pay the toll to enter the village.” The highest level among the group came forward. He was level 9 while the rest were all level 5 and above.


Really? Save a girl and get surrounded by a group of thugs.


Could it get any more cliché?

“Don’t look so upset. It’s an initiative we have taken to collect money to help the players who have difficulty in surviving. We can take anything, Gold, Weapons, food anything. Anything you can donate. The more the better.” He continued with a patronising smile.

“We have just started this today. It still hasn’t spread yet.” He replied unbothered.

“Now, now, don’t be in such a hurry. We are just doing this for the good of the players in need. Surely you can contribute something.” He grabbed my shoulder.


“I have nothing to contribute. If you are so keen on helping them, do it yourself.” I shrugged his hands off.


“You are being very selfish now, Mister.” The others surrounding us brought out their weapons.


“If you want to help the people, call for everyone in the Village and announce such things. We cannot trust just anyone to carry out such things. Everyone needs to decide it first.” Veena tried to mediate.


“Are you calling us liars, lady? Can’t you see the ten of us? Don’t we look like players in need?” A gruff looking man, level 8, I noticed walked up to her, trying to intimidate her. I noticed that she didn’t even blink an eye. Instead, I saw her eyes harden a bit.

There is a level difference of twenty one between me and their highest level. My stats should be around 4 times more than their strongest member.


“So be it then.” He brought out his sword and pointed it directly towards me. “Bring out the quest rewards you got from completing some high level quests for the old shopkeeper. No use lying. And since you‘re being such an uncooperative brat, bring out your gold and equipment too.” he poked the sword towards me.


“And the girl can pay for herself. I think I know what I want from her.” the gruff one grinned looking at her.

The next second, I brought out my sword from my inventory and slashed the man who was holding me at sword point, taking off his arm. Before anything could happen, I threw chilli bombs towards the suddenly frozen group while my sword arm moved, thrusting the sword through the man’s head.

She grabbed the hand of the man who was going to touch her face, twist it around and, bringing out a dagger from her inventory, repeatedly stabbed the man in the eyes and skull. The man might only have been one level short of her but that was a genuine martial arts move and the guy barely got any time to react. His face was already covered with the same powder she had thrown on the piglets.

The dead bodies however didn't disappear immediately and after I had killed everyone, I faced the reality of the situation.


Dulled emotions or not, when the adrenaline faded away, I collapsed onto my knees as the realisation dawned on me. My breaths were becoming shorter and I had the urge to throw up when I recalled the feeling of slashing through them with my sword.

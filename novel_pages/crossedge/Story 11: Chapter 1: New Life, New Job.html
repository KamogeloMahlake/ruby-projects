This made the girl in front of me giggle and I couldn’t help but blush at the untimely rebellion of my stomach. In my defence, the food looked awesome.


“Then don’t mind if I take a bite.” I thanked her and picked up the chopsticks and took a bite of the food. “Oh.”


“How is it? Did you like it, Sensei?” She asked me.


“This is amazing.” I took another bite.


“Really? I am pleased that you like my cooking.” She smiled.


“Wait what! You cooked this?” I asked, surprised.


Seriously, was this girl a genius chef or did I just suck that bad at cooking?


“Yes. I cook my bento myself.” She nodded.


“That’s….you are seriously talented, Miss Himejima. This is probably the second best cooking I have eaten in my whole life.” I praised.


“What only second? Who is the first then? Your girlfriend?” She teased.


“I rolled my eyes. “Interested in those things already? Then again, I guess you are at that age now. But for your answer, no. My favourite cook is my mother.” I smiled.


“Oh. You are very close to your Okaa-sama, Sensei?”


“Yes. She has always supported me in my dreams. I would be an ungrateful twerp if I didn’t like them.” I replied as I took another bite of the food. “This is seriously good. You would make your future husband a lucky man, Miss Himejima.”


“Ara ara.” She laughed with a hand on her cheeks.


I finished half of her food without realising while we talked when another person entered the room.


We looked back and it was Akeno who spoke. “Rias!”


“Akeno and….sensei?” She asked surprised and then looked around the room. “What are you doing here, Akeno?” She asked.


“Oh, I saw sensei reading alone in the library and decided to talk to him. Turns out that sensei didn’t have his lunch today so I offered him some of mine.”


“Sensei didn’t have lunch?” She asked, suddenly looking at me.


“Ah….I didn’t have time to buy something today.” I answered awkwardly. The incident from yesterday came to the forefront of my mind again.


I tried not to let it bother me as the girl sat down in front of me, beside Akeno and put up a bento box on the table. “Then sensei, please have some of mine too. I was offered some cookies from some juniors and I don’t think I will be able to eat everything.” She opened the box and slid it towards me.


This box, while not even close to Akeno’s bento, was also good.


Now, I was already eating one student’s lunch so it would be awkward if I rejected hers….thinking of this, I, a bit hesitantly took a bite of her food.


As I ate the food, I saw the girl’s face brighten up.


Ignore it.


The girls talked to me for a while and it was almost the end of the lunch break so we decided to leave.


On the way back, I heard some commotion and saw a group of students chasing three boys with shinai in their hands.


What the hell?!


Are they fighting on school premises?


I quickly ran there to see what was going on and the two girls followed me.


The girls had the three boys surrounded between them and it looked like they were going to give them the beating of their lifetime.


“What is going on here?” I asked, stopping the girls from attacking as they looked towards me.


“Furukawa-Sensei!”


I looked at the brown haired girl. “Murayama, correct? What is going on here? What are you girls doing? Why are you attacking these boys?” I asked, seriously. This was the first time I had encountered this kind of behaviour in this school.


I remembered these students.


They were mostly first years.


“Sensei, these….these perverts were peeping at us in the changing room.”


“What!” I was taken aback at the accusation and looked at the three boys who were scared shit at the moment.


“Are you sure?” I asked. These kinds of allegations were quite serious. It was better to confirm it before taking action.


“Yes Sensei. It’s not the first time either. This year alone, it’s the sixth time we have caught them doing so. Who knows how many times they have escaped without being caught.”


“....hah!” I was actually stunned and at a loss of words. “Sixth time?!”


“Yes.”


“Did you girls complain to the teachers?”


“We told the Student Council and they said they would take care of the matter but these three always return back to their usual behaviour. They are infamous in the school as the Perverted Trio.”


….


I felt a vein bulge on my forehead at the words of the girls.


“I see. Don’t worry. Let me take care of them. Also, I understand the anger and frustration of you all for the breach of your privacy but please, resorting to violence is not the way society works. Don’t degrade yourself for this.” I reassured them.


“Yes Sensei.” The girls looked a bit reluctant but still moved back.


I walked in front of the boys and I realised that one of them was not looking at me even as I stood in front of him and was instead staring behind me. It took me a moment to realise that he was looking at Rias and Akeno.


“Boobs.”


….


Did this guy….just say what I think he did?


Looking at the reaction of the girls, especially as Rias crossed her arms a bit defensively while the rest looked at them as if they were lower than bugs, it seems that I was not mishearing things.


This confirms it. These guys….they are really the culprits.


I moved slightly, standing right in front of the two girls, covering them from his gaze and looked at the enraged students.


“I promise that I will take care of this matter. You all are here to learn with the peace of your mind. If you don’t feel protected here, it’s the fault of us teachers. I will definitely give you all a satisfactory answer.” I reassured them again.


“Thank you, Sensei.”


I turned towards both Rias and Akeno. “Thank you for today but I have to take care of this matter first.”


“Please take your time, sensei.” They nodded and moved aside. 


Finally looking at the three guys, I ordered in a cold tone. “Follow me.”


The first place I led them was to the Teacher’s office and asked around if any of the teacher’s knew about the whole mess they had been creating.


It turns out that the teacher’s didn’t know everything and had left everything to the Student Council because the Student Council President had reassured them that she could take care of this matter….which seemed utterly stupid.


As this was not even the first offence, I had their parents called over to the school and personally had them suspended for a month.


Even their parents seemed to have given up.


I felt kinda sorry for them but then again, if they couldn’t even teach their kids basic manners, then some fault for it lies in them as well.


Next up, I called for one of the staff to help fix the place where the fools were peeping from. Seriously, how was there a hole in the wall and if so, why does no one fix it?


Finally, when everything was done, it was time to deal with the Student Council.


I was seriously pissed at these brats.


I knocked at the door of the Student Council Room and the door was opened by the girl who had shown me to the Principal’s office on my first day. Tsubaki Shinra.


“Sensei?”


“Is Miss Shitori in there? I have something to talk to her about.”


“Oh, yes. Please come in.”


The office looked like a teacher’s room. In fact, it was actually better than the teacher’s room. What even was the work of the Student Council for them to need something like this at their disposal?


But let’s not get off topic.


The students in the room stood up and greeted me and I nodded back.


“I have had a very tiring day today so let’s get straight to the point, Miss Shitori. What did the Student Council do to deal with the case of the three first year boys who were caught peeping on the girls of the Kendo Club?”


They were taken aback by my question but answered nonetheless.


“They were punished by extra duty after school.” She answered, straightening her glasses, something which was unnecessary as it was already perfectly in place.


“I assume that was the first time you encountered this problem. While a bit light, I accept your reasoning. Then what about the next four times?”


“Excuse me?”


“What did you do the next four times they were caught doing the same. I was informed that the students had complained to the Student Council. Did you get the complaints or were they lying?” I asked, staring at her, looking for any hints of lies.


“They were punished accordingly.” She said after some time.


“‘Accordingly’ is a very flexible word, Miss Shitori. It varies from person to person. I would like to know the exact details of their punishment because if they were not bothered to correct their behaviour, it was evident that your methods didn’t work. Did you inform the teachers about this?” I asked.


“I….took permission from the teachers and they allowed me to deal with the matter.” She defended.


“How many times?”


“What?”


“I was only told that you informed them once. Only once were they told that such a thing had happened on the school premises. I think you misunderstood something. When he gave you the permission to deal with this matter, it was only for that once. Then what of the next four times. Why did you not inform the teachers of this matter?”


“I….I assure you….”


“Assurances don’t run the school, Miss Shitori. Actions do. I have heard some good things about you from the teachers so I will give you this advice. You are here to learn, so learn. If something is beyond your capabilities, ask the person in charge to deal with it. Knowing when to pull back is also a skill necessary in management. In this case, you, by not going to the required authority on time, the problem which could have been solved much earlier lasted longer. Tell me, are you perhaps friends with the boys?” I asked.


I had thought of several reasons why the incidents were never reported and this was one of the top ones.


“Furukawa-sensei, are you insinuating that I am covering for the boys?” She asked hotly.


“Well yes. Look at it from my position and tell me if that doesn’t sound probable?” I asked. “Or if you have any better reason, give me one?”


“I didn’t want to escalate the matter to not affect the reputation of the school and ruin the lives of those boys.” She replied, angry at the accusation but this reply just made the vein in my head throb even more.


I looked at her and tried to control my temper and was finding it extremely hard to do so. “Tell me something, Miss Shitori….”


“Yes.”

“Are you stupid!” I bursted out. They were taken aback at my outburst but I didn’t give them any chances of taking back and went on.


“You think covering for those fools is saving the reputation of the school?! Peeping is a crime punishable by law in this country, do you know that?” I asked and saw her mouth opening but she closed it again. “Well, do you?”


She nodded.


“Then pray tell me, what would happen if these same students got the impression that their behaviour was not that serious, and started doing this outside the school and got caught? Or would those girls chasing and beating other students in broad daylight be a good thing? Would that bring great laurels to the school’s reputation?”


Her mouth opened like a fish and was about to reply but I raised my hand and shut her up.


“And pray tell me this, if the problem continues like it has been, do you think the girls of the Kendo Club or any others who were the victims of their crimes are just going to keep quiet? That they won’t complain to their parents and the word that Kuoh Academy has a deplorable security for girls won’t spread? There are so many things wrong with your reasoning that I could stand here all day and lecture you and we still won’t be done.”


I took a deep breath calming myself down.


“I was told by some teachers that you are planning to open a school of your own and wanted to learn management by the means of this position. Well, if this is your attitude then you would perhaps make for a great businesswoman but I promise you that you would be an incredibly shitty educator.”


“Sensei!”

“What, Miss Shitori? Am I wrong?” I challenged and she actually backed down. “You were selected by the votes of the students of this school, correct?” She nodded.


“They selected you because they trusted you enough to protect them and help maintain a safe environment for them to learn. But here you are completely ignoring the very students who put their faith in you….to do whatever it is that you were doing. You did nothing to solve their problems. Hell, you all probably didn’t even investigate this matter. Those three were peeping through a hole in the changing room….all six times from the same place. The least you could have done was get someone to get it fixed. Sigh….what a disappointment.”


She looked down and I observed that she had her fists clenched and there were actually tears in the corner of her eyes.


I shook my head and left the room leaving the Student Council in silence.


The day’s events exhausted me.

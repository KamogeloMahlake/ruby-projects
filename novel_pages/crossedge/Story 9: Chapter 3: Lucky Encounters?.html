I wasn’t sure if they would be rigged.


I would rather buy them later on by myself.


But I had more important things to think about.


In these two weeks, I had locked myself in a room and practised Transfiguration Magic with all my might and I had completed the first three years of the Transfiguration Spells taught in the school.


The Diadem of Rowena Ravenclaw helped a lot in doing so.


I still had two more weeks before I would start teaching so I was preparing to complete studying the books and theory.


There were some differences in the spells when I used them compared to what was written in the books.


My guess was that it was due to my Demonic Power affecting the spells.


I will have to test this with other people before I start teaching.


Just as I was thinking of these things, I sensed a summoning.


I hadn’t been able to give much time to contracts in these last two weeks except the regular talks with the Emperor.


I fixed things around myself to respond to the summoning.


A familiar scene greeted me on the other side of the portal.


Albus Dumbledore.


“Oh my, what a surprise. What do you need me for today, Albus Dumbledore?”


He threw a shrunk trunk towards me which I accepted and enlarged before opening to check its contents.


It was the remaining payment. Bottles of Luck Potion, gold as well as books on many topics of magic, five vials of Phoenix Tears, one Phoenix Feather and the best part of it all, the trunk itself. The space inside it was huge.


I had asked him for a case similar to Newt Scamander’s.


“Well, what do you know? You did get all the payment. We are even now.” I smiled at the guy.


He hesitated a bit.


“Oh ho, I am sensing another deal here.”


He stiffened a bit and looked at me and nodded.


“Go on, state what you desire.”


“I… wish to know what you know about Sirius Black’s involvement in the deaths of James and Lily Potter and a way to prove his innocence if he is uninvolved.”


“Sirius? Yes, I do know. Then, what do I get in return?”


“Tell your price.”


“Hmm… let me think.”


I thought of the plan and an idea struck me.


“How about… you teach me everything you know about Transfiguration and Alchemy and I will even throw in another offer and remove the soul fragment from little Harry..”


….


“Excuse me?” The man looked confused, not expecting such a request so I clarified.


“I’ll be honest, surprising for a devil but what can I do? I was roped into teaching some humans about Human Transfiguration Magic but I have no idea about teaching someone so… you teach me. The things I do for a deal.”


….


“You… wish to learn… to teach others?”


“It’s a deal and I don’t skive on my side of a bargain. You should know that better than anyone else. While it’s not my place if they transfigure themselves into pigs, it would still leave a bad taste in my mouth and a stain on my reputation. I won’t have any of that.”


The man looked stunned, still trying to figure out what he was hearing.


My eyes twitched at his reaction.


“So, do you agree or not?” I impatiently asked for a reply.


He broke out of his stupor and nodded his head, still confused.


“Great then. Every day, you will teach me for an hour minimum regarding Transfiguration. Everything you know about it.”


I smiled and continued. “As for Sirius Black. The guy is innocent. They had changed the secret keeper after you left. The actual secret keeper was Peter Pettigrew. Sirius went mad with grief since he was the one who had suggested the change and thought he had killed his friends. As for how to save him… Peter Pettigrew is still alive. He has been hiding as a rat of the Wesley Family since then and must be in Hogwarts Castle right now, sleeping beside the boy whose parents he betrayed. Go on. Finish that first and then come back and teach me.”


I ignored his shocked reaction and closed the portal.


Well, that solved the problem of learning Transfiguration at a good enough speed without fumbling around with books.


I was wondering what I would do next when I heard a knock on my door. With a wave of my wand, things were packed away and the trunk shrunk as I hung it from my neck.


On opening the door, a familiar face appeared in front of me.


Lavinia Reni.


“Ace, I have come to play again.”


….


During this time, Lavinia had often visited me.


I found out that she had recently joined the organisation after her teacher was killed by Augusta of the Wizards of Oz. She wanted to find and kill that witch to avenge her teacher.


The girl was feeling quite lonely as there wasn’t anyone she knew in this place and I was the only one around her age who she talked to.


Most of the students around our age found her weird and maintained their distance. Sometimes children can be such bastards.


I don’t know what the situation was in this world as not everything was the same as the world from the novels. Hell, even the Lucifer was a woman in this world instead of the one I knew.


But Lavinia’s teacher in my memories… had actually abandoned her willingly and was actually one of the members of the Wizards of Oz. If that was really the case here, she would be a broken mess.


The girl was too pure and optimistic. I almost felt bad thinking about such a situation.


“Were you busy?” She asked while making herself comfortable on my bed already, looking at me with pleading eyes.


Sigh.


I waved my wand and a pillow was transfigured into a bunny.


Her eyes lit up and she hugged the bunny instantly and began playing with it.


She loved to play with the animals which I transfigured for practice.


“Did you make a new spell today?” She asked.


“Hmm. Just tried something different.” I replied and put aside my wand and got up to take a break. “How were your classes?”


“Yes. It was good. I was taught ice magic.” She took out her wand and created a snowflake on its tip to show me. “Amazing, right?”


I smiled at her expectant gaze. “Yes. Very amazing.”


It was quite refreshing to have someone like her around.


Till now, only Dave was someone I had trusted and even then it took me a few years to fully trust him but Lavinia had somehow burrowed her way into my life very quickly.


Man… Dave must be worried too.


I want to send him a message somehow but I am not sure if it will be tracked and they will find a trace about my real identity.


Till now, I was using the Gaea Foundation to keep up my disguise when outside.


It did consume my power but the consumption wasn’t high enough to be a problem. Not to mention, it was a good way to train my reserves too.


Overall, I was getting fairly used to my new life in this place.


____________________________________x_________________________________


Two more weeks had passed.


The time was more or less uneventful except the fact that I had started my lessons with Dumbledore from the next day.


And I have to say that the man was a genuine master of the subject.


I learned more in a single hour with him that I had learned in all this time by myself. The Ravenclaw’s Diadem helped as well but the man’s talent was without a doubt.


I am pretty sure that if he became a Devil, he could easily rise to an Ultimate Class level in time.


With two weeks of continuous lessons, I could do all transfiguration spells till the fourth year syllabus and was halfway through the fifth year things.


My progress was quite frightening if I do say so myself.


Still, it was enough for me to conduct a lecture for people who had no idea about the subject.


I won’t be making a fool of myself in class tomorrow.


Apart from that, my talks with the Emperor were going smoothly. I used my conversations to push some thoughts into his head, making him doubt Honest and think more cautiously.


With enough time, I am sure I can get him to clean up that bastard.


The phone in my room rang and I picked up the call.


“Ace. Come to my office. We need to discuss a few things before your first lecture tomorrow.” It was Mephisto’s voice on the other end of the call.


“Okay.”


I got ready and went to his office and knocked.


“Come in.”


Mephisto was alone in his office, doing some paperwork. He looked up at me and spoke. “Take a seat. I was just working on the paperwork for your class.”


I listened.


“As you know, your magic is quite… rare. Not completely unheard of but extremely rare and as such it has never been taught before. Tomorrow, you will be giving a presentation in front of your crowd. From that, people can choose to sign up for your lectures. How many people you can get is all on you so give it your all.”


I nodded.


- Mephisto Pheles -


He sat in front of the lecture hall, waiting for the lecture to start as he recalled the things he had been able to find about the boy in all this time.


It took him several spells to find out that he was always under a disguise and the name he gave was probably a lie too.


The only thing which was true was his age.


He wondered just what kind of life a kid like him had lived to develop such an attitude.


That said, from his one month observation, he could assess that the boy didn’t have any ill intentions towards other people and just wanted to grow stronger on his own.


He wanted to become someone who didn’t have to suffer like the Low Class Devils did.


Honestly, this goal was something he could appreciate rather than some kind of foolish rebellion which would have only gotten him killed.


Thankfully, it seemed he was getting close to Lavinia.


He had been a bit worried when she hadn’t been accepted by the other students at the organisation due to her….unique character.


Anyways, getting a wielder of a Longinus to side with the organisation would be a great boon and if he could get a talented magician as well, that’s just a cherry on the cake.


It was actually funny that the boy hadn’t even realised how rare his own magic was all this time.


He was doing something which only a handful of people could do and probably something nearing the level of the Annihilation Maker in a sense.


What was the scope of this magic?


What was the scope of the boy himself?


There is always a very fierce competition between the different Magician Organisations and he didn’t want to accept it but in terms of top level talents, his Organisation was lacking a bit compared to the Golden Dawn and Rosenkreuzer.


Faust was a rare talent but while they had strong people in Grauzauberer, they were not geniuses like Mcgregor Mathews or Rudiger Rosenkreuz.


But he hoped that Ace would change this.


Ace entered the room, dressed in a formal magician robe, a black wand on his side.


“Welcome everyone. My name is Ace and I will be presenting to everyone a magic of my own creation. I call it Transfiguration Magic. It is a branch of magic that focuses on the alteration of the form or appearance of an object, animal or person, via the alteration of their molecular structure. I call it magic but it is just as much science as it is magic.” He poured himself a glass of water, took a sip of it and kept the rest on the table in front of him and waved his wand.


The glass morphed into a bird in front of the amazed eyes of everyone.


“I had once heard someone say that billions of years ago, the whole universe was formed from gas clouds. So, everything that exists today was once the same thing. In other words, it should be possible to change things between each other. This was my basic thought when making this magic and this is the result. And it’s not as if this hadn’t existed before. Alchemy uses this concept as well. It’s just that there has never been a magic which can directly transfigure things without the complicated alchemical processes." He tapped the bird with the wand and it turned into a towel which he used to wipe his wand and then dropped it on the ground, waving the wand at it, making a chair before taking a seat.


“Transfiguration is a very systematic, exact magical discipline, working best for the scientifically-inclined mind and, as such, is exceptionally hard work, especially in comparison to other forms of magic. When transfiguring, it is important to make firm and decisive wand movements. Do not wiggle or twirl your wand unnecessarily, or the Transfiguration will certainly be unsuccessful.”


The entire room had gone silent and everyone was just gawking at the boy in front of them.


“Before we proceed, let me warn you all. This is an extremely dangerous branch of magic. You could die a million ways from a single glass of water if you mess up the transfiguration so don’t be hasty and join my classes at your own risk. If anyone doesn’t have the resolve to pursue this, do both yourself and me a favour….leave the class and don’t waste our time.”


He stared at them for a while.


No one among the three hundred and fifty people left, their eyes stuck to the stage.


Mephisto smiled.

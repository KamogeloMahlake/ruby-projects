So, it was obviously a bit unnerving when someone who could overturn this entire situation just by taking a casual stroll through the streets of New Lilith, was inviting them to a party. This wasn't even a choice at this point for them. Not like they would reject the invitation anyway.


It was an opportunity to make better relationships with him and if that wasn't possible, then know more about their side.


Thus she decided to join the meeting. Grandmother Gremory and Lord Gremory were coming to talk about the yearly spar which was a whole other mess they didn't want to delve into at this point of time.


She looked at the name and address of the Bar and couldn't help but let out a chuckle. "At least he has a good sense of humour."


The scene in front of her was not something she was expecting from a bar opened by Lucifer.


There were obviously the Youkai but other people were completely unexpected.


"Is that Odin? Thor? Does the boy have a connection with the Norse?"


And not only the Norse. The one sitting beside Odin and leering at Yasaka who was promptly ignoring the duo made her lips twitch.


Obviously he would know. He had always been good at digging out information. And the guy had the audacity to disturb them about Rias' Boosted Gear. She would bet that he was laughing at them when he did so. It was just like that bastard.


"Welcome. I am glad you all could make it here." Her thoughts were broken by a voice sounding beside her. She turned around and was frozen at the sight of the person standing there.


Grandmother Gremory and Lord Gremory were the same.


Sirzechs was the one least affected because he had already encountered this situation before. "Good to see you too, Damien. Thank you for inviting us. By the way, these are Serafall, the incharge of Foreign Affairs and the one currently holding the position of the Leviathan. Beside her is my Grandmother, Chysis Gremory and my Father, Zeoticus Gremory."


"Welcome to my little piece of Hell hahaha. Also, no need to watch that Leviathan title around me. That would be for the Leviathan clan but then again it's not like you all took their permission when you started using the names. So why are you hesitating now?"


She winced internally at the comment. Grandmother Gremory and Lord Gremory did the same. Especially Chysis. As someone who had seen and fought under the Original Satan's, to hear this from someone who was basically a living image of the Devil… It must be hard on a whole other level.


They had taken those titles as means to reassure the common people that they still had Satans. To give them some measure of moral support. Was it a low blow to the losing side? Most likely. But it did help them calm the general people to some extent.


Devils were a long lived race and there were still too many older devils alive even among the low and middle class ones who would not accept any other leader than the Satans ruling over the Underworld.


Was it completely selfless? Not at all. There were several older pillar devils who relished in the power of having a say about these names. Everyone knew this. It was just not spoken.


So, when faced with such a statement, it was obvious to dig up some guilty conscience of a devil of the older generation like Chysis.


"Come, take a seat. I am still waiting for some guests but it seems they will be late." He said as he led them to the table which was occupied by the leaders.


They took their seats and she could see the surprised look on Azazel's face. So he didn't know that they were invited too. The look however turned into a smug one and now she wanted to bash his face again.


"Serafall, Sirzechs, what a surprise to see you both here." He greeted us.


"Azazel. You knew?" Sirzechs asked.


“Knew? About this meeting? Well obviously. I think you forgot a very common fact. Damien and I are family.”


A family who have fought a war spanning throughout the world. Then again, wasn’t the Great War, just a glorified family feud between the three factions?


“You seem to get along well with him.” Sirzechs spoke as a purple haired Fallen Angel served drinks to all of them. A Fallen Angel?


“Oh Celine. You are working here too?” Shemhazai asked the waitress, surprised.


“Yes. I liked the atmosphere here. I play the guitar for the show.”


“Hahahaha you were always good with the harp back in heaven. It’s good to see you taking back to playing instruments.”


“She is?” She couldn’t help but ask, seeing the Vice-Governor of the Fallen Angels being so familiar with her.


But the answer came from someone else entirely.


“Celine of the Holy Flames. It was thought that you had perished back in the Great War because you had disappeared. To think I would see you after so long.” Grandmother Gremory said.


“You are… the little girl from the Gremory who followed that bratty devil. Yes, this is indeed a surprise. How is your ancestor doing? Still playing around with those camels of hers?” The Fallen Angel replied.


….


….


Now, even she knew that there were very few people who would call the first Gremory by such a name so it was evident that this Fallen was someone pretty high up and seeing the unphased reaction meant that her words weren’t something new.


Sometimes even she, as a Satan, forgot how ancient most of these beings were.


“Yes. She’s just retired now.”


“Hmm. Tell her to drop by someday. It would be good to talk to her under different circumstances.” She replied. “If you will excuse me. I have a show to perform now.”


“Sure. It was… good to see you too, Celine.”


“Yes. It was not exactly bad.”


“Mother, she was?” Lord Gremory inquired, just as curious as them.


“Celine, the Daughter of Archangel Uriel. She was one of the most talented Angels back during the Great War, under only the Seraphs and rivalling even them in terms of power. Both of our Ancestors have fought her several times without any results during the Great War, especially yours Serafall whose powers were completely opposite to hers.” She looked at her before saying the last part. “And then after the Great War, news about her suddenly stopped as if she had disappeared suddenly. Not something uncommon during those times. “I didn’t know she had fallen. But why is she working for a Lucifer?”


Uriel had a daughter? She hadn’t heard about it.


Shemhazai sighed. “She was caught in an ambush and fell. Heaven rejected her. When she came down, she was captured by the Old Satan faction and had been imprisoned there for centuries. Damien found and freed her along with several hundred other Fallen Angel captives. She was barely a living corpse when he found her. He treated her, got her any kind of help she needed to properly heal her mind. By the end of it, she decided to live with him.”


“They had prisoners of war? I thought it was decided to free prisoners from all sides when we decided to stop the war.” She asked. They had actually freed a lot of them after coming into power. One of the reasons why none of the factions attacked them at their weakest.


“We didn’t have any ways to know who was dead and who was alive.” Shemhazai let out a sigh.


“I would have thought that she would have joined the Fallen Angels rather than devils especially after her time in captivity.” Grandmother Gremory said.


“The ambush which caused her to fall… It was done by one of the Fallen Angels. Penemue is already out for blood. The whole matter was such a mess.”


“Ah… that’s… yes. Those were not good times.”

“Still, I didn’t think you all would have such good relations with Damien, you know, what with him being a Lucifer and all that. It’s just surprising.” She said.


At this point, Shemhazai looked at her and then smiled. “Tell me Serafall, what do you all know about Lucifer? And I mean Lucifer before his rebellion? I will not lie, Lucifer as a devil was scary. He was insane. A twisted version of what we knew of him. But… do you know how or what Lucifer was like before his rebellion?


She shook her head.


“Helel was the brightest Angel of Heaven. Dad’s favourite. In fact, compared to him, Michael was basically a middle child. He was strong and charismatic. Someone at whom most of us looked up to. Hahahaha, not many know of this but He was the next in line for the position of the King of Heaven if Dad ever retired.”


He laughed, making her mouth open in shock.


“Yes, that brat was Yahweh’s loyal attack dog. Always ready to wipe out any heretic or pagan God as they say. Old times. I personally like the new one better.” Odin remarked.


“To be honest, if he had rebelled and stayed the way he was, then even as a Fallen Angel, at least a third of Heaven would have followed him but… he didn’t fall.”


“What?”


“Yes. It’s a very less known fact. He was the only one who didn’t fall. No. He left voluntarily. The rest of us were kicked out.” He laughed at their confused look. 


“Do you know why despite being against Dad, we never banded together? Why are there three factions instead of just Heaven and the rebels? Well, a part of it was that whatever he had become, we were scared of him. The very presence of The Devil repulsed us. But a large part of it was also Jealousy. How can he throw a tantrum and still not fall while we were kicked out of heaven for a small mistake? How could he reject what we craved for but couldn’t get? We were jealous and angry and the new Lucifer didn’t make it easier. Seeing the bright and loving brother of ours turn into that… that abomination… we were just angry. So yes. We were just being kids. And you already know how kids sort things out between themselves?”


“And Damien?”


“He is exactly what Lucifer would have been if he hadn’t become what he had.”

There was a reminiscing look in his eyes and no one among them disturbed his thoughts. But it did give them a whole new insight into the man who created their race.


All their thoughts were attracted by the shifting stage as three figures appeared on the stage. One of them was the one they had just met, Fallen Angel Celine. The other two were different. A man who looked to be in his twenties with long white hair sat on the piano. He seemed like a normal human but Lord Gremory and Grandmother Gremory seemed to have different opinions.


“Is that?”


“Yes.” Azazel smirked.


That man was probably here just to laugh at them.


“But how? As far as we knew he would never ally himself with Devils.”


“With normal devils, maybe not. But as Damien says, he is ‘The Devil’. Even we don’t know how he did it.”


“Who is he, father?” Sirzechs asked, taking another look at the man.


“Merlin.”


"Merlin… you mean that Merlin?" She asked, taking another look at the man.


“Then again, he’s half brothers with Damien. They get along pretty well. I thought you’d be more interested in the last one.” Azazel commented, as they looked at the last person. The girl who was the singer of the group. Light purple hair, golden eyes. She looked to be in her teens, maybe sixteen.


It was when she tried to feel her power that she got chills. Not just her. All three of them. But this girl was still heads and shoulders above them all.


“Who is she?” She asked, incredulously. Just how many of these monsters did this guy have? This would make seven or eight already. And each one of them was stronger than her.


This girl… she was almost approaching the levels of Ajuka and Sirzechs. Another one of those and one who was with the new Lucifer.


As if realising something, she looked around and checked everyone in the building and the observation sent chills down her spine. Not a single staff member was below Ultimate Class level in strength.


There were over a hundred staff in the entire building. The stronger ones were beyond Satan Class.


Azazel took this chance to throw another bomb as if all the existing ones were not enough already.  


“Ingvild Leviathan.”


Fuck!


Suddenly something struck her. Is that why he laughed when Sirzechs introduced her?


He knew she would find out about her today.


She turned towards Sirzechs. “But didn’t you say…”


“Yes. I was also under the impression that she had fallen under the Sleeping sickness." He replied and looked at Azazel who raised his hands in surrender. "I don't know anything about that. It's totally on Damien."


Could it be? Did he find a cure for the Sleeping sickness?


If that was so then….


The performance started but it didn’t reach her as she was too busy trying to comprehend the situation.


While this was happening, a blue haired boy walked up to Azazel and spoke. “Normally we have a no pictures rule in this bar but Boss has decided to make an exception for you today for a million dollars. Will you take it?”


Shemhazai almost spit out his beer at the offer and even she looked incredulously at him. What kind of insane idiot would take that offer?


Although a million dollars is nothing for Supernaturals like them, it was still quite dumb.


The man boy added. “The Boss says you will not regret it.”


- Azazel -

… obviously he was that crazy. It was The Devil. Was there even a doubt?


He brought out his mobile and transferred the amount without question. “I want to welcome them myself.”


Sean brought out a camera from his pocket and handed it to him. “Obviously, Boss knew you would say so already. Have fun.”

“Yes. He left a year ago but comes back once in a while.”


“Hmm. Still, to think he would get that of all things. It seems that Damien values him a lot. The Child of Light and the Lightbringer hahahaha. Even their names match.”

“That Spear of your Father. The boy had it.”


*Crash*


The glass in Serafall’s hand fell down and crashed into the ground.


Even he was surprised at the reveal and took another look at the boy who was waiting at the tables.


Damien was hiding too many surprises it seems.

“Where are you going, Azazel?” Behind him, Shemhazai asked.


“Just receiving someone.”

Six days had passed since they had come to the Land of Flowers for their mission.


Her team’s mission was to infiltrate the Daimyo’s mansion and discover the information about the new farming methods, the formula for the fertilisers and finally the special seeds which the Daimyo’s lands used to grow their crops.


The information about the miraculous growth of the crops in the Land of Flowers had spread to the neighbouring lands as well and everyone wanted to know how they could do such a thing.


Obviously finding the information was quite easy as the fool had spread the information among every common farmer.


That said, they soon noticed a difference.


While the produce in the other lands increased as well, they were still lower than the produce of the Daimyo’s lands.


It was then guessed that the Daimyo must be using some other secret method for his lands which further increased the production of crops.


Her mission this time was to find this method and slip away with the procession of the Princess of the Land of Stairs.


Obviously the Daimyos of the Land of Stairs and her own Daimyo must have some kind of agreement behind the scenes which she was not aware of.


But her question was not asking questions but completing the mission.


It had taken her three days to figure out the locations where the Prince worked and three more days to verify the entire place. Tomorrow they were scheduled to leave and today was the day they had to work everything out.


An unusually large warehouse a little distance away from the main Palace. There was also a school and a martial arts dojo combined with the place.


Late at night, after the royal families had gone to sleep, she and her team left their rooms and vanished in the darkness of the night, gathering in the forest in the outskirts, looking at the building from a distance away.


The building looked quiet and they checked for anyone around the place but didn’t find anything except the guards on the gates and some guards making rounds around the place. None of them went inside the place.


From the information she had found out, only the Prince and two girls who were thought to be his lovers were allowed inside.


The boy apparently had two or three lovers, one of them being the samurai girl following him. Still, what Daimyo didn’t?


At least the boy was far more attractive than any other Daimyo she had seen. Hell, she had felt her heart skip a beat the first time she had seen him.


That Princess was lucky to get a shot with this hottie instead of being married off to some fat bastard.


Still, this is also what made her sure that the secrets of the Prince’s research were obviously inside this place.


With a signal from her, all of them rushed inside the building from several directions.


Each of them took a different route to ensure they could check the whole buildings at the fastest speed possible.


She herself headed towards the most important rooms, like the Principal’s Room and other official rooms but didn’t find anything inside them.


It was when she was about to leave that she noticed another courtyard which looked like living rooms, perhaps the quarters for the teachers.


Did the teachers go on a vacation because she couldn’t see anything there?


She went ahead and found the rooms indeed looked like people were living there until recently. She checked around and found a locked door to the basement.


To her surprise, the room’s doors were actually reinforced steel under the wooden outer layer. Instead of frowning, her eyes lit up.


Was it this place?


Obviously the room was locked with several locks but these things were as simple as flicking a finger for her to unlock. Behind the door were stairs which went below. She lit up a matchstick and slowly walked down carefully before she stopped. A thin wire shining under the light of the matchstick.


Trap.


It was well hidden and could probably catch even inexperienced Chunin off guard but she was a Jōnin and it would be stupid for her to fall for something like this.


The trap only served to make her more excited as she was sure that this place had something to hide.


She checked the place and simply walked on the walls to avoid the traps until she reached below to another door.


This one had more complicated locks and even took fifteen minutes to unlock.


“Damn that Flower Boy has gone beyond his level to protect this place.” She muttered.


On opening the room, she found it to be outlined with books everywhere.


There was a desk with paper and many shelves with what looked like containers full of seeds. Jackpot.


She quickly brought out the sealing scrolls and began storing everything inside.


“You missed one on the bottom shelf.” Suddenly a voice made her freeze. On reflex, she reached for her kunai but found herself frozen. No matter how much she tried, she couldn’t move a muscle.


She heard footsteps behind her as the figure came in front and she saw the face.


“Prince Kazuya!”


The boy didn’t show any expression but brought spoke paper seals and placed it on her and then a handcuff and cuffing her hands, finally making a hand seal.


She found herself losing control of her chakra.


This is...the chakra suppression seal!


After that, the boy brought an injection from his pocket and injected her with some kind of liquid.


Poison.


Dammit!


She felt her muscles relax and drowsiness trying to settle in.


Quickly thinking of a plan, she pretended to sleep while forcing herself to stay awake. This poison must be strong because she was from the Land of Medicine and had high resistance to poisons and yet she felt the effects. If it were any normal shinobi, they would have fallen asleep already.


This was a bet. It all depended on if her ploy worked.


If he catches her, she dies, if he doesn’t, she escapes.


She waited and thankfully, the boy seemed to be assured and she felt the paralysis fall as she felt the boy’s hands on her back, grabbing a hold of her collar to pick her up.


Using this opportunity, she bit her tongue, forcing her to push away the drowsiness, turned and took a hold of the boy between her thighs and in a quick motion of her hands, she snapped his neck.


Yes.


A smile came to her face.


Even without chakra, the power of a Jōnin was not something people like these could contend with.


Sure, the Prince dying would be a big problem but that has nothing to do with her.


Maybe someone didn’t want the relationship between the two Lands and assassinated the Prince.


She has already gotten what she had come here for and will leave early tomorrow morning along with the rest of the envoy.


These thoughts of hers however crashed as the figure between her legs...burst into a cloud of smoke.


“Bunshin!”


The paralysis on her body was activated again and several voices came from behind him.


“See, I told you, it wouldn’t be so easy.” A girl’s voice came and she raised her head to look at the figure of the samurai girl who always followed the boy.


Beside him, the boy was rubbing his neck and was frowning.


“Well, I know. Why do you think I was using a clone?”


“Still, you could have paid more attention.”


“Kuina-chan is not wrong, Your Highness.” Another girl’s voice came as she looked at three figures climbing down the stairs. To her horror, each one of them had one of her team members with them, all knocked unconscious and chakra sealed.


“Underestimating ninja, vampire or human, is always foolish.” Another voice came and the speaker was a girl with black hair and green eyes, someone she hadn’t seen before.


“It seems you need to increase your training more, Your Highness.” The rough looking man in the group said.


“Ah, I took measures, didn’t I? Stop picking on me already.” The boy complained as he looked at her. “Hello, Mogami-san or should I call you Tsukimi Kodon?” The boy smiled, his eyes shining in the dark, suddenly sending shivers down her spine.


- Kazuya -


I chucked the entire team in different cells, this time, making it so that they couldn’t even move properly if I didn’t want it.


Seriously, I had to thank Tamami for helping me out with the seals or I would be stuck without any options against such kinds of attacks.


Paralysis Seals and Chakra Sealing Seals were extremely useful for us.


“What should we do now?” Asked Saitō. “Because there is no way they would ignore the missing ninja in their envoy considering their true intention of bringing them here.”


“I am not intending to let them ignore it either.” I said, taking out one of the kunai I had confiscated from them and playing with it.


While these people were investigating me, I was doing the same for them.


I found out the information about the Jōnin just this morning. Six days for the information was understandable considering she was just a common Jōnin from a small village without any special skills.


From this, I found out about her mission.


The Medicine Daimyo was really pushing his limits now so it was time to bring him down a notch.


“Set explosions in several places of this warehouse as well as set off an alarm. Kill one of the chunin from their team and spread the news that they tried to rob the warehouse and attacked me. Even stole fifty pounds of gold and several bits of research data while destroying the rest.” I commanded.


“We can forge the identities of the chunin as well as reveal the identity of the Jōnin, putting a bounty on their head.” Saitō added and I nodded.


“Yes and I will have Grandpa and the Stairs Daimyo put some extra pressure on the Medicine guy to drive away the Kodon Clan from his land.”


“Do you think it will work? I mean the Medicine Daimyo can deny any involvement too.”


“Oh he sure could, but he will not. That guy is counting a lot on this marriage. After all, it’s his long term plan to take over the Land of Flowers. He will absolutely take action even if it's only to satiate our anger. After all, there is no way he would give up potentially acquiring both our lands over one small ninja clan.”


“But wouldn’t that put the Clan against us?” Kanae asked.


“They are bound to go against us, after all, the clan protecting the seal of Rōen is the Kodon Clan. We have to face them sooner or later so it’s better to have them removed from the Land of Medicine sooner rather than later.”


In fact, even if it weren’t the Kodon Clan, I would have found a way to pin this on them and have them removed from the Land of Medicine. They were a Clan with three Jōnin and several Chunin level ninja which would also serve to cut a bit of the Medicine Daimyo’s power.


This attack had turned into an unexpected boon for us.


Especially since it gave me the extra chunin level ninja I needed for my next ritual.


“But how would we fool them? After all, you are unhurt.” Kuina asked.


“Oh you will see that soon.”


We quickly got ready to arrange the scene according to our requirements.


When everything was done, I made a hand sign and detonated the whole place.


“Now for your question, Kuina.” I took the kunai I had been playing with and quickly stabbed myself in the shoulder, taking all of them by surprise. “What are you saying? I think I am plenty hurt.” I replied through gritted teeth.


Dammit, that hurt.


“Kazuya! Are you insane?” She quickly shouted and hurried to me.


I was bleeding and in moments, my entire haori was dyed red.


They helped me remove my kunai and bandage while Saitō took another kunai and stabbed himself and even cut himself at different places as well. At their confused looks, he replied. “Someone has to save our untrained Prince and make them retreat. I am just a common samurai from their point of view so being unhurt against a Jōnin and possibly other chunin could make it suspicious especially when I was protecting someone like him.” He explained.


They helped us treat our wounds while many others quickly rushed.


An explosion so close to the palace would attract anyone’s attention after all.


Let the drama begin.
